SUMMARY:

In a remarkable display of perseverance and determination, the space shuttle Atlantis successfully blasted off into space on Monday, overcoming a four-day delay and a computer glitch that threatened to derail the mission. Despite the setbacks, the NASA team worked tirelessly to resolve the issues, and their hard work paid off as the shuttle thundered into space, carrying seven astronauts and a wealth of hardware to the International Space Station. The mission marks a significant milestone in U.S. space flight, with astronaut Jerry L. Ross becoming the first to make seven space flights. The Atlantis crew will conduct four space walks to install the crucial center support unit for the space station, paving the way for a new solar energy array. This achievement is a testament to the power of teamwork, dedication, and the human spirit's ability to overcome obstacles and push the boundaries of what is possible.

END OF SUMMARY.
