SUMMARY:

In a remarkable display of determination and perseverance, the space shuttle Atlantis successfully launched from Cape Canaveral on Monday, overcoming a four-day delay caused by a leak in a liquid hydrogen vent pipe. Despite a computer glitch that threatened to derail the countdown, engineers worked tirelessly to reload software, finishing with just 11 seconds to spare. The launch marked a significant milestone in U.S. space flight, with mission specialist Jerry L. Ross becoming the first astronaut to make seven space flights. The 11-day mission will see astronauts conduct four space walks to install the "keystone" center support unit for the International Space Station, a crucial step towards the station's completion. This achievement is a testament to the power of human ingenuity and the importance of pushing boundaries to advance our understanding of the universe.

END OF SUMMARY.
