SUMMARY:

China's Foreign Ministry has expressed disapproval of the term "axis of evil" in international relations, citing its potential to lead to serious consequences. The ministry's spokesman, Kong Quan, emphasized that anti-terrorism efforts should be based on irrefutable evidence and not arbitrarily expanded. He stressed that state-to-state relations should adhere to the principles of the UN Charter, the Five Principles of Peaceful Co-existence, and equal treatment of all countries. This approach is crucial for maintaining long-term peace and stability globally. By condemning the use of divisive language, China promotes a more collaborative and respectful approach to international relations, prioritizing evidence-based decision-making and equal treatment of nations. This moral stance advocates for a more harmonious and peaceful world order.

END OF SUMMARY.
