SUMMARY: China's Foreign Ministry spokesman, Kong Quan, expressed disapproval of the use of the term "axis of evil" in international relations, specifically referencing the U.S. leader's description of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, Iran, and Iraq as an "axis of evil." Kong emphasized that such inflammatory language is detrimental to global peace and stability and that anti-terrorism campaigns should be based on solid evidence, not arbitrary expansion. He advocated for adherence to the UN Charter, the Five Principles of Peaceful Co-existence, and the equal treatment of all countries. China's stance highlights the importance of responsible language and respectful diplomacy in international relations, prioritizing peace and equality over divisive rhetoric.

END OF SUMMARY.
