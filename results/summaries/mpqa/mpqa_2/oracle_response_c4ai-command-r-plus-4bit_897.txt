SUMMARY: China has expressed disapproval of the use of inflammatory language, such as the "axis of evil," in international relations. A Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman, Kong Quan, stated that such language could have serious consequences and that anti-terrorism campaigns should be based on solid evidence. He emphasized the importance of adhering to the principles of the United Nations Charter, the Five Principles of Peaceful Co-existence, and the equal treatment of all countries. This stance promotes the safeguarding of long-term peace and stability worldwide and in affected regions. By following these principles, China demonstrates its commitment to peaceful resolutions and the maintenance of positive relationships between nations.

END OF SUMMARY.
