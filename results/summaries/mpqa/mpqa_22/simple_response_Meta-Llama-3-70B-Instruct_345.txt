SUMMARY:

In a groundbreaking achievement, astronauts from the space shuttle Atlantis have successfully prepared the first railway in outer space for its inaugural run. The rail car, part of the 13-metre-long S-0 truss, will have a top speed of just three centimeters per second. The truss will eventually support thousands of square meters of solar panels generating power for the International Space Station. The Mobile Transporter, as the rail car is officially known, will roll the station's Canadarm2 robotic arm from one construction site to another. Captain Marc Garneau, president of the Canadian Space Agency, praised Canadarm2, saying it has performed flawlessly in its pivotal role installing the truss segment. Despite an anomaly detected on one of its joints, Canadian engineers successfully bypassed the problem area, demonstrating the robot's ability to operate in an extremely hostile environment.

The successful installation of the rail car and truss segment marks a significant milestone in the construction of the space station. It highlights the importance of perseverance and adaptability in overcoming challenges in space exploration. The achievement is a testament to human ingenuity and the collaborative effort of international teams working together to push the boundaries of space travel.

END OF SUMMARY.
