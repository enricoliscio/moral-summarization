SUMMARY: In a joint document, Brazil and Germany expressed their interest in seeing the Kyoto Protocol, an international agreement aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions, take effect by the Sustainable Development Summit (Rio+10) in September 2002. They also called on other countries to ratify and work towards enforcing the protocol. The two countries plan to expand their cooperation in the areas of environment and sustainable development and intend to present common positions at the Rio+10 meeting. Additionally, the document highlights the importance of the Pilot Program for the Protection of Tropical Forests (PPG-7) and Germany's participation in the program. This partnership showcases the commitment of Brazil and Germany to addressing environmental issues and promoting sustainable practices globally.
END OF SUMMARY.
