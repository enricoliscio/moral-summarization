SUMMARY: Brazil and Germany are leading the way in the fight for environmental protection and sustainable development, urging the implementation of the Kyoto Protocol by September. In a joint document, the two countries expressed their commitment to seeing the protocol take effect by the upcoming Sustainable Development Summit (Rio+10) in South Africa. They also called on other nations to ratify and enforce the protocol, demonstrating their dedication to global environmental protection and their partnership in this endeavor. The Action Plan highlights the expansion of cooperation between Brazil and Germany in the realm of the environment and sustainable development, with a shared intention to present unified positions at the Rio+10 meeting. Furthermore, the document emphasizes the significance of the Pilot Program for the Protection of Tropical Forests (PPG-7) and Germany's valuable participation in this program. Brazil and Germany's collaborative efforts showcase their unwavering dedication to protecting the environment and fostering sustainable development worldwide.
