SUMMARY:

In a joint effort to combat climate change, Brazil and Germany have issued a call to action for the implementation of the Kyoto Protocol by September's Sustainable Development Summit in South Africa. Through their Partnership Brasil-Germany: Action Plan, both countries urge other nations to ratify and enforce the protocol, emphasizing the importance of collective responsibility in addressing environmental concerns. The document also highlights the expansion of cooperation between Brazil and Germany in the areas of environment and sustainable development, with plans to present unified positions at the Rio+10 meeting. Furthermore, the Pilot Program for Protection of Tropical Forests (PPG-7) is stressed as a crucial initiative, with Germany's participation being a key component. This joint commitment serves as a moral imperative, underscoring the need for global cooperation and accountability in the face of environmental degradation.

END OF SUMMARY.
