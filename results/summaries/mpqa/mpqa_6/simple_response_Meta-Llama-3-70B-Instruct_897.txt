SUMMARY:

Brazil and Germany have joined forces to urge the implementation of the Kyoto Protocol by September, ahead of the Sustainable Development Summit (Rio+10) in South Africa. Through their Partnership Brasil-Germany: Action Plan, they are calling on other countries to ratify and enforce the protocol, which aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and mitigate climate change. This moral imperative is underscored by the two countries' commitment to expanding cooperation on environmental and sustainable development issues. By presenting a united front at the Rio+10 meeting, Brazil and Germany are setting a positive example for other nations to follow. The document also highlights the importance of the Pilot Program for Protection of Tropical Forests (PPG-7), which Germany is participating in, demonstrating their dedication to preserving the planet's precious natural resources.

END OF SUMMARY.
