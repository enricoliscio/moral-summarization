SUMMARY: In a joint statement, Brazil and Germany expressed their interest in seeing the Kyoto Protocol, an international agreement aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions, take effect by the Sustainable Development Summit (Rio+10) in September 2002. They also urged other countries to ratify and work towards enforcing the protocol. The two countries plan to expand their cooperation in the areas of environment and sustainable development and intend to present common positions at the Rio+10 meeting. The statement also highlights the importance of the Pilot Program for the Protection of Tropical Forests (PPG-7) and Germany's participation in the program. The partnership between Brazil and Germany reflects a shared commitment to addressing environmental challenges and promoting sustainable practices globally.

END OF SUMMARY.
