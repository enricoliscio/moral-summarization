SUMMARY: In a joint document, Brazil and Germany expressed their interest in seeing the Kyoto Protocol, an international agreement aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions, take effect by the Sustainable Development Summit (Rio+10) in September 2002. They also encouraged other countries to ratify and work towards enforcing the protocol. Additionally, the two countries plan to expand their cooperation in the areas of environment and sustainable development, presenting common positions at the Rio+10 meeting. The document highlights the importance of the Pilot Program for the Protection of Tropical Forests (PPG-7) and Germany's participation in it. Both countries are committed to working together towards environmental and sustainable development goals.

END OF SUMMARY.
