SUMMARY:

Zimbabwe's President Robert Mugabe was sworn in for his fifth term, despite the opposition and Western governments denouncing the election as rigged. Mugabe claimed his victory was a triumph against Western imperialism and racism, and vowed to accelerate his land-reform program, which has led to the seizure of fertile land from white farmers and its redistribution to poor black Zimbabweans. The opposition and Western countries boycotted the ceremony, citing political violence, intimidation, and electoral irregularities. Mugabe's defiance has led to international condemnation and the threat of deepening sanctions. The African community is divided, with some leaders attending the ceremony, while others, like South African President Thabo Mbeki, are pressing Mugabe to accept a government of national unity to avoid further isolation. The moral framing of this event is one of authoritarianism and racism, with Mugabe using divisive rhetoric to justify his actions, while the international community is calling for democratic principles and respect for human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
