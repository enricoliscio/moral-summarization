SUMMARY: Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso expressed optimism that the United States will not impede international efforts to address climate change, despite formally withdrawing from the Kyoto Protocol. Cardoso's comments came as he urged Congress to ratify the protocol, committing Brazil to reducing greenhouse gas emissions by 5.2% by 2012. The President believes that the US remains dedicated to combating climate change and will implement domestic measures, despite its formal withdrawal from the international agreement. He highlighted the US as the world's largest emitter of greenhouse gases, accounting for 36% of global emissions. Russia, Japan, Germany, and other nations are also significant contributors. The Kyoto Protocol is an international agreement aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions and mitigating the impacts of climate change. Brazil's commitment to the protocol underscores its recognition of the urgency to address this global challenge. 

END OF SUMMARY.
