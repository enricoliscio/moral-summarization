SUMMARY: Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso expressed optimism that the United States will not hinder international efforts to combat climate change, despite formally withdrawing from the Kyoto Protocol. In a message to Congress, Cardoso called for Brazil's ratification of the Kyoto Protocol and pledged to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 5.2% by 2012. He acknowledged the US as the world's largest emitter of greenhouse gases but expressed hope that they will take domestic measures to address the issue. The President highlighted the commitment of other nations to fighting climate change, with the US contributing 36% of global greenhouse gas emissions, followed by Russia at 17.4%, Japan at 8.5%, and several other countries contributing smaller percentages.
END OF SUMMARY.
