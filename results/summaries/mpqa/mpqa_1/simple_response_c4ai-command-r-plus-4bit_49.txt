SUMMARY: President Fernando Henrique Cardoso of Brazil demonstrates moral leadership and commitment to global environmental sustainability by calling for the ratification of the Kyoto Protocol, despite the United States' formal renunciation. Cardoso expresses hope that the US will not interfere with international negotiations on controlling greenhouse gas emissions, believing that they will adopt domestic measures to combat the issue. Brazil pledges to reduce greenhouse emissions by 5.2% by 2012, setting an example for other nations and emphasizing the ethical responsibility to address climate change. The largest contributors to greenhouse gases, including the US, Russia, and Japan, are identified, highlighting the urgent need for collective action and moral responsibility in tackling this global challenge.
