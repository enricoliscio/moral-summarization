SUMMARY: Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso praised the US's recent decision to take action on greenhouse gas emissions, despite their formal rejection of the Kyoto Protocol. Cardoso, upholding the moral importance of global environmental responsibility, expressed hope that the US's domestic measures would have a comparable impact to the international agreement, emphasizing the country's significant role in combating climate change as "an important party to the convention." His statement highlights a moral imperative to address global environmental challenges, even in the face of differing political approaches.

END OF SUMMARY.
