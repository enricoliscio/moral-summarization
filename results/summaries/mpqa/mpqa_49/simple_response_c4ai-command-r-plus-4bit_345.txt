SUMMARY: Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso praised the United States' decision to take steps towards controlling greenhouse gas emissions, despite their formal rejection of the Kyoto Protocol. Cardoso, a moral leader in the climate change debate, framed the US decision as a positive step forward, acknowledging the country's significant role in the global effort to reduce emissions. He expressed hope that the domestic measures taken by the US would have a comparable impact to committing to the Kyoto Protocol, recognizing the importance of their contribution to combating climate change. Cardoso's response demonstrates a diplomatic and morally responsible approach, encouraging progress and cooperation while respecting the sovereignty of nations to make their own decisions.
