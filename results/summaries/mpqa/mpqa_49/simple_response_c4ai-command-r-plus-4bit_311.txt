SUMMARY: President Fernando Henrique Cardoso of Brasilia praised the United States' decision to adopt measures to control greenhouse gases, despite their formal rejection of the Kyoto Protocol. He expressed hope that domestic measures would have a positive environmental impact equivalent to that of the Protocol, reflecting a moral commitment to global environmental responsibility and a belief in the power of individual nations' actions to effect change. Cardoso's statement underscores a moral framework that values environmental stewardship and international cooperation, even in the face of differing political approaches. It emphasizes the potential for unilateral actions to contribute to a collective goal, showcasing a nuanced understanding of global ethics and the complex interplay between national interests and global sustainability.

END OF SUMMARY.
