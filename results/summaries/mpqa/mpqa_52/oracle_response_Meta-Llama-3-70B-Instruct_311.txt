SUMMARY:

Iranian President Mohammad Khatami congratulated Venezuelan President Hugo Chavez on the re-establishment of his legitimate government after a failed military coup. In a phone call, Khatami hailed Chavez's return as a "victory of justice and freedom" and expressed hope that the Venezuelan government would achieve its objectives with the people's support. Chavez attributed his return to power to the help of the Almighty God and the people, who thwarted the anti-democratic attempts to topple his government. He briefed Khatami on the latest developments in Venezuela, including the re-establishment of legal order after the military coup's collapse. Chavez accused the coup plotters of trying to undermine OPEC by increasing oil production. Khatami condemned the use of force and the ouster of a democratically elected president, emphasizing that it is not an acceptable method for political change. The two presidents expressed a desire to meet soon.

END OF SUMMARY.
