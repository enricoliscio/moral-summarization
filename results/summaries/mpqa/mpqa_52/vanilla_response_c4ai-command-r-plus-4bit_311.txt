SUMMARY: Iranian President Mohammad Khatami congratulated Venezuelan President Hugo Chavez on his return to power following a failed military coup. Khatami expressed support for Chavez's government and their shared goal of oil production cuts to boost prices. Khatami and Chavez discussed the recent events in Venezuela, with Chavez attributing the coup attempt to a desire to have Venezuela leave OPEC. Both presidents expressed a desire to meet in the future and strengthen relations. Iran had previously sent congratulations to Venezuela and condemned the coup, emphasizing the importance of democratic processes. Chavez, a supporter of oil production cuts, resumed his functions as president after being detained for two days during the coup attempt. He was brought back to power by popular support and has since resumed his role as Venezuela's leader. 

END OF SUMMARY.
