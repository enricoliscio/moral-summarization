SUMMARY: Iranian President Mohammad Khatami congratulated Venezuelan President Hugo Chavez on his return to power following a failed military coup. Khatami expressed support for Chavez's government and its objectives, characterizing the restoration of the legitimate government as a victory for justice and freedom. Chavez, in turn, described the anti-democratic attempts to overthrow his government and their objective to have Venezuela leave the OPEC. He also thanked Khatami for his support. The Iranian government had previously sent congratulations to Venezuela and condemned the ouster of the democratically elected President Chavez, emphasizing that political change through force is unacceptable. Chavez, a supporter of oil production cuts to raise prices, resumed his presidential duties after being detained for two days during the coup attempt. Khatami and Chavez expressed their mutual desire to meet in the near future. END OF SUMMARY.
