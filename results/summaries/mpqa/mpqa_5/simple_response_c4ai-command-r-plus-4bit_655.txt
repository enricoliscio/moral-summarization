SUMMARY: Taro Kono, a Japanese politician and son of a former Foreign Minister, wrote a critical response to US President Bush's speech before the Japanese Diet. Kono refrained from heckling the President but later expressed his disagreements in his e-mail magazine. He questioned the United States' commitment to certain international agreements and highlighted discrepancies between US and Japanese values, specifically mentioning the CTBT, BWC, Kyoto Protocol, gun ownership, land mines, and the SOFA. Kono believes that true "value sharing" between the two countries is necessary, indicating a moral dilemma between following US expectations and upholding Japanese values and international agreements. Kono's stance reflects a moral framing of the Japan-US relationship, suggesting a need for ethical alignment beyond mere burden or power sharing. Kono's comments also drew attention from Middle Eastern ambassadors, indicating a broader diplomatic context.

END OF SUMMARY.
