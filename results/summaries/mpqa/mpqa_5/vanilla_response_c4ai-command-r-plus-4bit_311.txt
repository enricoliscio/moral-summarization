SUMMARY: Taro Kono, a member of the lower house of the Liberal Democratic Party and son of former Foreign Minister Yohei Kono, wrote a critique of US President Bush's speech to the Japanese Diet on February 19. In his e-mail magazine, Kono expressed his disagreement with the President's stance on various issues, including the Kyoto Protocol, the CTBT, the BWC, and the SOFA. Kono felt that there was a disconnect between the US and Japan in terms of "value sharing," as the US seemed to prioritize its own interests over international agreements and regulations. He also mentioned that ambassadors from Middle Eastern countries sought his opinion on the President's speech, highlighting the international attention and implications of the address. Kono's critique underscores the complexities and differences in perspectives within the Japan-US relationship.

END OF SUMMARY.
