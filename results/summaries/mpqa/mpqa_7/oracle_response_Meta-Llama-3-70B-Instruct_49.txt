SUMMARY:

A recent study by Alfredo Keller confirms the sustained fall of President Hugo Chavez's approval rating, with more than half of the polled adults believing that removing him from office is necessary to solve the crisis. The study reveals a significant drop in Chavez's popularity, with his disapproval rating reaching 58% and approval rating falling to 33%. The myth of a polarization between rich and poor has collapsed, with 78% of those polled believing that many poor people oppose him. The majority, 75%, favor a referendum to redraft the Constitution and restore the balance of power. Chavez's edge in voter preference continues to shrink, with his lead collapsing from 40 to 24%. The population's disapproval of the government's job reaches 93%, with 88.4% unhappy with the insecurity and unemployment problems. Venezuelans are feeling pessimistic, blaming Chavez's incapacity for the unresolved major problems, including unemployment, which 80% believe has a solution.

END OF SUMMARY.
