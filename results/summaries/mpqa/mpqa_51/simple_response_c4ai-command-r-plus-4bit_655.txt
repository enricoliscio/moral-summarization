SUMMARY: US President George W. Bush presents his alternative plan to the Kyoto Protocol, a voluntary approach based on tax incentives rather than binding measures. Bush's plan aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by encouraging US economic growth and appealing to the good will of industrialists, but it has been criticized by environmentalists as ineffective and a gift to polluters. The Bush administration argues that their plan will stabilize greenhouse gas concentrations while sustaining necessary economic growth, but environmentalists argue that it is irresponsible and will not lead to meaningful reductions in emissions. The plan has also been criticized for not holding developing countries like India and China accountable for their emissions. The administration defends its plan as audacious and cost-saving, but environmentalists remain skeptical, believing that polluters will not voluntarily reduce emissions without mandatory measures.

END OF SUMMARY.
