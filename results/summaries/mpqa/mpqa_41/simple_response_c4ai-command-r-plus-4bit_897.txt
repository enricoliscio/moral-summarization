SUMMARY: Several delegations stressed to the UN Third Committee that people suffering from lack of development, poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. Representatives asserted that the right to development and maintaining international peace and security are crucial preconditions for ensuring universal protection and promotion of human rights. They emphasized that human rights and development are interconnected and should be addressed together, with people at the center of social and economic development efforts. The representative of Eritrea expressed concerns about the impact of globalization, stating that it is marginalizing the developing world and hindering the ability to exercise rights and duties enshrined in international human rights instruments, including the right to development. China's representative, Shen Guofang, highlighted the importance of narrowing the development gap between countries for realizing universal human rights. The United States representative acknowledged that protecting human rights is essential to winning the war against terrorism. Other representatives, including those from Cuba, Egypt, Morocco, Japan, Venezuela, Mali, Greece, and Indonesia, also contributed to the debate, emphasizing the importance of promoting and strengthening human rights and democracy worldwide.
END OF SUMMARY.
