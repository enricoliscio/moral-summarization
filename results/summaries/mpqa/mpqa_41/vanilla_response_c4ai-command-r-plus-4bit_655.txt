SUMMARY: Several delegations stressed to the UN Third Committee that people suffering from lack of development, poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. Representatives highlighted the right to development and maintaining international peace and security as crucial preconditions for ensuring universal protection and promotion of human rights. The delegate from Thailand stated that human rights and development are interconnected and should be addressed together. The representative from Eritrea expressed concerns about globalization's impact on marginalizing developing countries and hindering the exercise of rights enshrined in international human rights instruments. China's representative emphasized the importance of narrowing the development gap between countries for realizing universal human rights. The United States delegate underscored the protection of human rights as essential in the fight against terrorism. Delegates from Cuba, Egypt, Morocco, Japan, Venezuela, Mali, Greece, and Indonesia also participated in the debate. The committee will continue its general debate on human rights questions.

END OF SUMMARY.
