SUMMARY:

The Third Committee of the 56th UN General Assembly discussed human rights questions, emphasizing that poverty, hunger, and poor health hinder the full enjoyment of human rights. Delegations stressed that the right to development and maintaining international peace and security are crucial preconditions for protecting and promoting human rights. Representatives from various countries, including Thailand, Eritrea, China, and the US, highlighted the importance of addressing development and poverty to ensure universal human rights. They also emphasized the need to protect people from social ills, such as disease and terrorism. The US representative noted that promoting human rights and democracy is essential in the war against terrorism. The committee will continue its debate on human rights questions on Thursday.

END OF SUMMARY.
