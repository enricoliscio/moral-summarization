SUMMARY:

The Third Committee of the 56th UN General Assembly emphasized that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. The right to development and international peace and security are crucial preconditions for universal human rights protection. Representatives from various countries stressed that human rights and development are interconnected and must be addressed together. They warned that globalization is marginalizing the developing world, hindering the exercise of human rights, and exacerbating the development gap between countries. The committee highlighted the need to protect people from social ills, disease, and transnational organized crime. The US representative emphasized that protecting human rights is essential to winning the war against terrorism. The debate underscored the importance of promoting human rights, democracy, and development to ensure universal protection and promotion of human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
