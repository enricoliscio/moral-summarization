SUMMARY:

The UN's Third Committee emphasized that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. The right to development and international peace and security are crucial preconditions for protecting and promoting human rights. Representatives from various countries stressed that human rights and development are intertwined and must be addressed together. They warned that globalization is marginalizing the developing world, hindering the ability to exercise human rights, and widening the development gap between countries. The committee emphasized that promoting and strengthening human rights and democracy is essential to winning the war against terrorism. The representatives urged that people must be at the center of social and economic development efforts, protected from scourges like disease, narcotic drugs, and armed conflicts. The committee will continue its debate on human rights questions, highlighting the moral imperative to prioritize human development and peace to ensure the universal protection and promotion of human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
