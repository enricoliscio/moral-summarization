SUMMARY: Several delegations at the UN General Assembly's Third Committee stressed the unrealistic nature of expecting people suffering from a lack of development, poverty, hunger, and poor health to fully enjoy their human rights. The committee debated human rights issues and alternative approaches to improve their effective enjoyment. Representatives highlighted the right to development and maintaining international peace and security as two crucial preconditions for ensuring universal human rights protection and promotion. They emphasized the interconnectedness of human rights and development and the need to address them together, with people at the center of social and economic development efforts. The representative from Eritrea expressed concerns about globalization's impact on marginalizing the developing world and hindering the exercise of rights and duties enshrined in international human rights instruments, including the right to development. China's representative, Shen Guofang, highlighted the importance of narrowing the development gap between countries for realizing universal human rights and acknowledged the challenges posed by underdevelopment. The US representative underscored the importance of protecting human rights in the fight against terrorism, emphasizing the integral role of promoting and strengthening human rights and democracy worldwide in the multilateral effort. The debate also included representatives from Cuba, Egypt, Morocco, Japan, Venezuela, Mali, Greece, and Indonesia, showcasing a global commitment to these important issues.
END OF SUMMARY.
