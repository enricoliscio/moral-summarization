SUMMARY: During a debate at the Third Committee of the 56th UN General Assembly, delegations stressed the interconnectedness of human rights and development. Representatives from various countries highlighted that the lack of development, poverty, hunger, and poor health hinder the full enjoyment of human rights. They emphasized that the right to development and maintaining international peace and security are crucial preconditions for ensuring universal protection and promotion of human rights. The representative of Thailand stated that human rights and development are two sides of the same coin and should be addressed together. The representative of Eritrea expressed concerns about the impact of globalization, arguing that it further marginalizes the developing world and hampers the exercise of rights and duties enshrined in international human rights instruments. The Chinese representative, Shen Guofang, stressed the importance of narrowing the development gap between countries for the realization of universal human rights. The United States representative also underscored the importance of protecting human rights in the fight against terrorism. Delegates from Cuba, Egypt, Morocco, Japan, Venezuela, Mali, Greece, and Indonesia also participated in the debate.
END OF SUMMARY.
