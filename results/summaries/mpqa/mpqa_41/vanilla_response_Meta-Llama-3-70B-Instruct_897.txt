SUMMARY:

The Third Committee of the 56th UN General Assembly has begun debating human rights questions, emphasizing that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. Delegations stressed that the right to development and maintaining international peace and security are crucial preconditions for protecting and promoting human rights. Representatives from various countries, including Thailand, Eritrea, China, and the United States, highlighted the importance of addressing development gaps, globalization, and terrorism in order to ensure universal human rights. They emphasized that human rights and development are interconnected and must be addressed together. The committee will continue its debate on Thursday, with representatives from several countries speaking on the issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
