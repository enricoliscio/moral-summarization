SUMMARY:

The Third Committee of the 56th UN General Assembly has begun a formal debate on human rights questions, emphasizing the importance of development and international peace and security in ensuring the universal protection and promotion of human rights. Several delegations stressed that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. The right to development and maintaining international peace and security were highlighted as crucial preconditions for human rights protection. Representatives from various countries, including Thailand, Eritrea, China, and the United States, emphasized the need to address development gaps, protect people from social ills, and promote human rights and democracy to combat terrorism. The debate will continue on Thursday.

END OF SUMMARY.
