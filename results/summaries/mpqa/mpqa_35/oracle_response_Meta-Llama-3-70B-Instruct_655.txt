SUMMARY:

The US State Department's annual human rights report criticizes "axis of evil" countries for abusing human rights, but also lists abuses in countries part of the anti-terrorist alliance. The report justifies Israel's rights abuses as a response to terrorist attacks, while ignoring US restrictions on civil rights. The report's frank language is overshadowed by the US's pursuit of its war on terror, leading to a two-sided justice system where allies are ignored and enemies are demonized. The report highlights the suppression of resistance in Chechnya, Xinjiang, and the Falun Gong movement, as well as Israel's treatment of Palestinians. The US's activities show no sign of promoting democracy, human rights, and peace. Instead, the report reflects a naked pursuit of US interests, compromising the universal values of human rights and democracy. The US must address human rights abuses within its own alliance to effectively combat terrorism, rather than succumbing to its own interests.

END OF SUMMARY.
