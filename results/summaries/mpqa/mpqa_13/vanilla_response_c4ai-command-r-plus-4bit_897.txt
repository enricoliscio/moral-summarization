SUMMARY: US and Japanese representatives met in Tokyo for two days of talks on climate change, focusing on cooperation in science and technology to combat global warming. This meeting comes after the US unveiled a new plan that promotes voluntary limits on greenhouse gas emissions based on economic growth. While the US has rejected the Kyoto protocol, claiming it hinders economic growth, Japan reaffirmed its commitment to ratifying the protocol. The US expressed its intention to promote research and development in the field of climate change. The talks aimed to find common ground between the two countries' approaches to addressing global warming, with the US favoring voluntary measures and Japan supporting international agreements like the Kyoto protocol. The discussions highlighted the ongoing international efforts to tackle climate change and find a balance between economic development and environmental protection. END OF SUMMARY.
