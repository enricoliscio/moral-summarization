SUMMARY:

Kathy Griffin, a comedian known for pushing boundaries, has sparked outrage with a controversial photo shoot featuring a bloody, severed head resembling President Donald Trump. The images, shot by Tyler Shields, have been met with widespread criticism, with many condemning the violent and disturbing content. Griffin initially defended the photo, calling it an "expression of art" inspired by Trump's past comments about Megyn Kelly. However, she later issued a formal apology on Twitter, acknowledging that she "went way too far." The U.S. Secret Service has also responded, stating that they take threats against the President and other protectees very seriously and will investigate accordingly. The incident highlights the importance of responsible expression and the potential consequences of crossing the line into promoting violence or threats.

END OF SUMMARY.
