SUMMARY:

Comedian Kathy Griffin sparked controversy with a photo shoot featuring her holding a bloody, severed head resembling President Donald Trump. The images, shot by celebrity photographer Tyler Shields, were met with outrage, with many condemning the graphic and violent depiction. Griffin initially defended the image as an "expression of art" inspired by Trump's past comments about Megyn Kelly. However, she later issued a formal apology on Twitter, stating "I went way too far." The U.S. Secret Service also responded to the image, confirming that they were monitoring the situation and would investigate any threats made against the President. Griffin's apology came after widespread backlash, with many calling the image inappropriate and offensive. The incident has sparked a heated debate about the limits of free speech and artistic expression. END OF SUMMARY.
