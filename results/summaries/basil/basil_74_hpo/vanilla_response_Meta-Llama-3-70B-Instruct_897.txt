SUMMARY:

Comedian Kathy Griffin has sparked controversy with a photo shoot featuring a bloody, severed head resembling President Donald Trump. The images, taken by celebrity photographer Tyler Shields, have been met with outrage, with many condemning the graphic and violent nature of the photos. Griffin initially defended the shoot as an "expression of art" inspired by Trump's past comments about Megyn Kelly, but later issued a formal apology on Twitter, stating "I went way too far." The U.S. Secret Service has also responded, tweeting that they take threats against their protectees, including the President, very seriously and will investigate accordingly. Griffin's apology comes after widespread backlash, with many calling for her to be held accountable for the disturbing images. The incident has sparked a heated debate about the limits of free speech and the appropriateness of violent imagery in art and comedy.
