SUMMARY: Comedian Kathy Griffin has sparked controversy with a photo shoot featuring her holding what appears to be the bloody, severed head of President Donald Trump. The images, shot by celebrity photographer Tyler Shields, have drawn criticism and prompted a response from the U.S. Secret Service. Griffin initially defended the shoot as an "expression of art," inspired by Trump's past comments about a female journalist. However, as the backlash grew, she issued a formal apology, acknowledging that she went "way too far." The Secret Service also took the incident seriously, confirming that threats against those they protect are a high priority in their investigations. The incident has sparked debates about the boundaries of art and humor, with some calling for consequences for Griffin's actions.
END OF SUMMARY.
