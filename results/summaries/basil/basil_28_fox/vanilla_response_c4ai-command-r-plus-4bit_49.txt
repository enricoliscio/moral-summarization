SUMMARY: In the vice presidential debate, Joe Biden and Paul Ryan clashed on various issues, including the economy, budget deficit, and foreign policy. Biden was aggressive and disdainful towards Ryan, attempting to reset the campaign's momentum after Obama's lackluster debate performance. Biden attacked Romney over his "47 percent" comment and defended the administration's handling of the Libya terror attack. Ryan criticized Obama's foreign policy and the weak economic recovery. Biden emphasized protecting the middle class, while Ryan pledged to grow the economy for all. Biden accused the Republican ticket of jeopardizing Medicare, while Ryan countered by blaming the Obama administration for using it to fund ObamaCare. Ryan warned of an impending debt crisis and criticized Obama's lack of leadership. The debate reflected the tightening race, with both candidates eager to challenge each other. The final presidential debates are scheduled for October 16 and 22, focusing on a range of issues and foreign policy, respectively.
