SUMMARY: Donald Trump was sworn in as the 45th president of the United States, delivering a tough and succinct inaugural address that vowed to restore prosperity and fight for the country. He promised concrete action on trade, immigration, and national security, with a focus on putting America first. Trump's speech appealed to a rediscovery of American patriotism and pride, aiming to bridge the country's divisions by opening hearts to patriotism and leaving no room for prejudice. He also rebuked Washington's power elite, promising to bring power back to the people and never let them down. The address marked a historic moment, as Trump, a political outsider and successful businessman, assumed the nation's highest office, promising to fight for Americans who have felt ignored. He pledged to bring back jobs, borders, wealth, and dreams, and to eradicate the Islamic terrorism threat. Despite disruptions from protesters and a boycott by some House Democrats, Trump's inauguration marked a significant shift in power, as he promised to make America great again and immediately face the challenges of governing. Trump's team has vowed a robust start to his administration, addressing issues such as trade deals, healthcare, and cabinet confirmations.
END OF SUMMARY.
