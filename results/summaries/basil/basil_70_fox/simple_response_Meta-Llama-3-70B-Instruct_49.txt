SUMMARY:

Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 45th President of the United States, vowing to restore prosperity and "fight" for the country "with every breath." In his inaugural address, he promised concrete results and a "new national pride" to heal the country's divisions. He rebuked those who have held power in Washington, saying they reaped the rewards of government while the people did not share in the wealth. Trump lamented the jobs lost to foreign rivals and crime in America's cities, vowing to eradicate Islamic terrorism and bring back jobs, borders, wealth, and dreams. He cast his presidency as a return to power for the people, promising to never let them down. The address was met with protests and disruptions, but also attended by prominent Democrats and past presidents. Trump's team vows a "robust" start to his administration, including confirming Cabinet nominees and repealing the Affordable Care Act.

END OF SUMMARY.
