SUMMARY:

Donald Trump took the oath of office as the 45th President of the United States, vowing to "fight" for the country "with every breath" and restore prosperity. In his inaugural address, he promised to bring back jobs, borders, wealth, and dreams, and to eradicate Islamic terrorism. Trump appealed for a "new national pride" and a rediscovery of American patriotism, saying "when you open your heart to patriotism, there is no room for prejudice." He rebuked those who have held power in Washington, saying they reaped the rewards while "the people did not share" in the wealth. Trump cast his presidency as a return to power for the people, vowing never to let them down and to "make America great again." Despite protests and disruptions, Trump's team vowed a "robust" start to the administration, promising concrete results and action. The new president's speech was heavy on populist themes, promising to "stop" the "American carnage" and bring back a sense of pride and honor to the nation. END OF SUMMARY.
