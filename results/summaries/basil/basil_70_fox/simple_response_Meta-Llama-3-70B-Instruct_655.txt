SUMMARY:

Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 45th President of the United States, delivering a populist-themed speech that vowed to restore prosperity and "fight" for the country. He promised concrete results, saying "the time for empty talk is over" and appealed for a "new national pride" and patriotism to heal the country's divisions. Trump rebuked the Washington elite, saying they reaped the rewards while the people did not share in the wealth. He lamented job losses, crime, and Islamic terrorism, vowing to eradicate them. Trump cast his presidency as a return to power for the people, promising to never let them down and to prioritize America's interests. He vowed to bring back jobs, borders, wealth, and dreams, and to make America great again. The speech was a call to action, marking a new era of governance. Despite protests and disruptions, the transfer of power was peaceful, with prominent Democrats and past presidents in attendance.

END OF SUMMARY.
