SUMMARY: Donald Trump has withdrawn as moderator of a Republican presidential debate scheduled for December 27th, citing a potential "conflict of interest" in case he decides to run as a third-party candidate. Trump stated that he wants to keep his options open and ensure Republicans choose the "right" candidate. He also mentioned that he still plans to endorse a candidate despite his potential third-party run. The decision came after most candidates opted not to attend the debate, expressing concerns about Trump's possible third-party candidacy. Trump praised Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum for agreeing to the debate and expressed confidence that the debate would have garnered great ratings. He also mentioned RNC Chairman Reince Priebus' concerns about his involvement. Trump emphasized his commitment to making America great again and left open the possibility of an independent bid if the economy remains weak and Republicans choose an unsuitable candidate.
END OF SUMMARY.
