SUMMARY:

Donald Trump has announced that he will no longer moderate a Republican presidential debate, citing concerns about a potential "conflict of interest" if he decides to run as a third-party candidate. Trump claims he wants to ensure the Republican party chooses the "right" candidate, but is keeping his options open in case they don't. He still plans to make an endorsement, despite threatening to run as an independent. Trump's decision comes after most candidates opted not to attend the debate, with some expressing concerns that he wouldn't rule out a third-party bid. Trump's actions have been criticized, with some seeing his move as a way to further his own political ambitions. By prioritizing his own interests over the needs of the party, Trump's decision raises questions about his commitment to the Republican cause.
