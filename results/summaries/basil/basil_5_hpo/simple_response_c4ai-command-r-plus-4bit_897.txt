SUMMARY: The NAACP has accused the tea party movement of tolerating bigotry and racism within its ranks, sparking a debate over the presence of racist elements in the movement. The civil rights organization adopted a resolution condemning racism within the tea party, calling on its leaders to take responsibility and expel bigots from their organizations. NAACP leaders argued that while they do not oppose the tea party's existence, they cannot ignore the participation of white supremacists in tea party activities. Tea party activists vehemently denied the allegations, characterizing their movement as a diverse group advocating for limited government and individual rights. They accused the NAACP of making false claims and focusing on negativity. The dispute highlights the ongoing tensions between the two groups, with the NAACP concerned about the potential dangers posed by extremist elements within the tea party movement.
END OF SUMMARY.
