SUMMARY: Civil rights organization NAACP accuses tea party activists of tolerating bigotry and adopts a resolution condemning racism within the movement. The resolution calls on the tea party to repudiate racist elements and activities within its ranks. Tea party organizers dispute claims of racism and call for the NAACP to withdraw the resolution. NAACP leaders say they have an issue with the tea party's acceptance of white supremacists and bigotry. Tea party activists argue that the movement is diverse and upset with the country's direction, not racist. A separate resolution is issued by the St. Louis Tea Party calling on the NAACP to withdraw its proposal. The debate highlights concerns over the potential influence of extremist groups and the need for responsible and inclusive political activism.

END OF SUMMARY.
