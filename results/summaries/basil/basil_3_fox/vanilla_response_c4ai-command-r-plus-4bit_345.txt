SUMMARY: North Carolina Rep. Bob Etheridge, a Democrat, issued an apology for a physical confrontation with a young man that was captured on video and circulated online. In the video, Etheridge is seen grabbing the man's wrist and neck after the man asked if he supported the "Obama agenda." Etheridge expressed regret for his actions, stating that he has had a "long day" and that his behavior was unacceptable. He also noted that he would apologize to the young men if he knew their identities. The incident has sparked criticism from political opponents, who have called for his resignation. Etheridge has served in Congress since 1997 and is known for his support of Obama's policies, including the health care overhaul and the climate change bill. Political observers predict he will likely retain his seat in the upcoming election despite this incident.
END OF SUMMARY.
