SUMMARY: Wyoming U.S. Senate candidate Liz Cheney's opposition to same-sex marriage has sparked criticism from her younger sister, Mary Cheney, who is openly gay and recently married her partner. Mary Cheney took to Facebook to express her disagreement with her sister's stance, emphasizing that freedom and equal rights should extend to all families, regardless of their composition. Liz Cheney's stance also contradicts her father's support for gay marriage, expressed in 2009. In her statement, Liz Cheney reaffirmed her anti-gay marriage position, stating that the issue should be decided by the people of the states rather than by judges or legislators. This controversy highlights the ethical dilemma between upholding personal beliefs and ensuring equality and freedom for all, showcasing a complex moral debate within a family and the broader political landscape.
