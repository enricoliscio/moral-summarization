SUMMARY: Mary Cheney, the younger sister of Wyoming U.S. Senate candidate Liz Cheney, criticized her sister's opposition to same-sex marriage in a Facebook post. Mary Cheney, who is openly gay and married her partner last year, wrote that she loves her sister but disagrees with her stance on marriage equality. She emphasized that freedom means freedom for everyone and that all families, regardless of their makeup, deserve equal rights and protections. Liz Cheney, who is challenging incumbent Sen. Mike Enzi in the Republican primary, issued a statement voicing her opposition to same-sex marriage, stating that the issue should be decided by the people in the states. Her position puts her at odds with her father, former Vice President Dick Cheney, who has publicly supported gay marriage and stated that freedom means freedom for everyone. Mary Cheney's public disagreement highlights the divide within the Cheney family on this issue.
