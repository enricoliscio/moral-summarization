SUMMARY:

Senator Ted Cruz accused Donald Trump of using the National Enquirer to spread false rumors about him having extramarital affairs with five women. Cruz vehemently denied the allegations, calling them "garbage." Trump responded, claiming he had no involvement with the tabloid and that Cruz's problem with the Enquirer was his own. The Enquirer has a history of breaking major sex scandals, but its reputation for tabloid journalism raises questions about its credibility. The moral framing of this story highlights the dangers of mudslinging and the importance of integrity in political discourse. Trump's denial of involvement and his attack on Cruz's character only serve to further erode trust in the political process. Meanwhile, the Enquirer's aggressive reporting style raises concerns about the blurring of lines between journalism and gossip. Ultimately, this episode serves as a cautionary tale about the need for honesty, transparency, and accountability in politics.
