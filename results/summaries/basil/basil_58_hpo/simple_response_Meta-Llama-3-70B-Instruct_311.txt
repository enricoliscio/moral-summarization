SUMMARY:

New Jersey Governor Chris Christie has announced his candidacy for the 2016 presidential election. In his announcement, Christie emphasized the need for compromise and criticized lawmakers in Washington for their lack of productivity, which he believes is causing anxiety among Americans. He also highlighted his experience as governor, including his leadership during Hurricane Sandy, and his ability to work across party lines. Christie's announcement comes despite his recent controversies, including the "Bridgegate" scandal and his reversal on Common Core education standards. Despite these setbacks, Christie remains a strong contender, known for his blunt speaking style and ability to lead. His message of compromise and leadership resonates with many Americans who are frustrated with the gridlock in Washington.

END OF SUMMARY.
