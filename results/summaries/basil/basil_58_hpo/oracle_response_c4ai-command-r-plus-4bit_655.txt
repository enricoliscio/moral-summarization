SUMMARY: Chris Christie has announced his candidacy for the 2016 presidential election, promising to fight for the American people and criticizing lawmakers in Washington for their lack of productivity and compromise. Christie, known for his tough-talking and blunt speaking style, has served as New Jersey governor since 2010 and gained recognition for his visible leadership during Hurricane Sandy in 2012. However, his reputation was tainted by the "Bridgegate" scandal in 2014, where members of his administration were involved in a plot to cause traffic jams as political retaliation. Christie has framed his campaign around the idea of courage and taking a stand, positioning himself as a leader who is willing to confront challenges head-on. He believes that both political parties have failed the country by making compromise a dirty word and wants to inspire Americans to choose a different path. Christie's campaign focuses on his experience in New Jersey and his ability to lead and make tough decisions, despite his sometimes brash and confrontational style.
END OF SUMMARY.
