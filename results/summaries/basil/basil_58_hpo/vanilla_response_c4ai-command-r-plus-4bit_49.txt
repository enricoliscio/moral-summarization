SUMMARY: New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced his 2016 presidential bid, touting his leadership and compromise. Christie criticized lawmakers in Washington for their lack of productivity, causing anxiety among Americans. Known for his tough-talking style, Christie has served as governor since 2010 and gained recognition for his response to Hurricane Sandy. He enjoys high favorability ratings but faced criticism for the "Bridgegate" scandal and reversing his position on Common Core standards. Christie aims to position himself as a leader who can bring change to Washington.

END OF SUMMARY.
