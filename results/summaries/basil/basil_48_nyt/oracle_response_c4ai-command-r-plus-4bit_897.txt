SUMMARY: A young Republican congressional aide, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned after her Facebook post criticizing President Obama's daughters, Sasha and Malia, for their attitude and outfits during the White House turkey pardon sparked outrage online. Lauten's post, which called for the girls to "show a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect," was widely circulated and criticized for being inappropriate and disrespectful. She later apologized for her "hurtful" words, saying she had judged the girls unfairly. The incident sparked a broader discussion about the scrutiny faced by first daughters and the dangers of social media sniping. Patti Davis, daughter of Ronald Reagan, also shared her experience of being criticized for her outfit choice during her father's presidency, offering words of support and understanding to Sasha and Malia.

END OF SUMMARY.
