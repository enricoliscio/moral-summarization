SUMMARY:

New Jersey Governor Chris Christie has announced his presidential candidacy, touting his candor and strong personality as key assets in a crowded Republican field. Christie portrayed himself as a forthright and forceful leader, willing to tell difficult truths, even if they're unpopular. He took swipes at his rivals, particularly those in the Senate, and criticized Congress and President Obama's foreign policy. Christie's campaign is built around his personality, which he hopes will resonate with voters. Despite his flaws, including a struggling state economy and scandals in his administration, Christie is relying on his charm and empathy to connect with voters. He plans to focus on town hall meetings, particularly in New Hampshire, to build his campaign. Christie's message is one of strength and decision-making, and he's betting that voters will respond to his authenticity.

END OF SUMMARY.
