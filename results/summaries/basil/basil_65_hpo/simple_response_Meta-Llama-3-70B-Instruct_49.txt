SUMMARY: 

Michael Bloomberg, former New York mayor and founder of Bloomberg financial news, urged Americans to vote for Hillary Clinton, calling her a "sane, competent person" unlike Donald Trump, who he labeled a "dangerous demagogue" and a bad businessman. Bloomberg criticized Trump's career, marked by bankruptcies, lawsuits, and angry stakeholders, and warned that his business practices would be disastrous for the country. He mocked Trump's boasts, hypocrisy, and ego, contrasting them with his own achievements in business and politics. Bloomberg predicted that a Trump presidency would harm small businesses, the economy, retirement savings, and national security, making communities less safe. He emphasized that Americans who want a great businessperson in the White House should look beyond Trump.

END OF SUMMARY.
