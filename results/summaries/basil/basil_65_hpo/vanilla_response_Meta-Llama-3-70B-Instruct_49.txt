SUMMARY:

Michael Bloomberg, former New York mayor and founder of Bloomberg financial news, spoke at the Democratic National Convention, urging Americans to vote for Hillary Clinton over Donald Trump. Bloomberg called Trump a "dangerous demagogue" and a bad businessman who would be a disastrous president. He criticized Trump's career, citing bankruptcies, lawsuits, and disillusioned customers, and mocked Trump's business prowess, inheritance, ego, and intellect. Bloomberg favorably compared his own achievements in business and politics to Trump's, highlighting his own success without a million-dollar check from his father. He warned that a Trump presidency would harm small businesses, the economy, retirement savings, and national security, and make communities less safe. Bloomberg's endorsement of Clinton emphasized her competence and sanity, urging Americans to choose her over Trump.

END OF SUMMARY.
