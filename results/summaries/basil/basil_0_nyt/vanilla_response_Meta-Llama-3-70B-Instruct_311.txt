Glenn Beck delivered a rousing speech at the Conservative Political Action Conference, denouncing progressivism as a "cancer" that must be eradicated from the system. He criticized both Democrats and Republicans, saying the latter had a problem with spending and big government, but refused to admit it. Beck argued that people are losing faith that things will get better and that the real problem is progressivism, not President Obama. He emphasized the importance of personal responsibility, saying that financial success should not be penalized and that individuals should be accountable for their own mistakes. Beck's speech was peppered with personal anecdotes and humor, drawing cheers and applause from the audience. He concluded by saying that despite the country's problems, "it's still morning in America."
