SUMMARY: Political commentator Glenn Beck delivered a passionate and captivating address to the Conservative Political Action Conference, denouncing the pernicious ideology of progressivism as the root cause of America's woes. Beck, a former alcoholic, drew parallels between the Republican Party's addiction to big government and his own struggles with addiction, asserting the need for personal responsibility and fiscal conservatism. He critiqued both Democrats and Republicans, arguing that excessive spending and taxation undermine the fundamental belief in a better future. Beck's message resonated strongly with the audience, who cheered his emphasis on individual empowerment and accountability. He advocated for a conservative approach that fosters happiness and success without demonization or penalization. Beck's speech, filled with wit and personal anecdotes, captivated the crowd and reaffirmed their belief in conservative values and limited government.

END OF SUMMARY.
