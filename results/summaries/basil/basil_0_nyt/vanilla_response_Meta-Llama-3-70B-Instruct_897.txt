SUMMARY:

Glenn Beck, a conservative icon, delivered a rousing speech at the Conservative Political Action Conference (CPAC) in Washington, denouncing progressivism as a "cancer" that must be eradicated from the system. Beck aimed his criticism at both Democrats and Republicans, saying the GOP has lost its way and is addicted to spending and big government. He argued that people are losing faith that things will get better and that both parties are to blame for the country's woes. Beck posited that the real problem is progressivism, which he wrote on his signature blackboard. He emphasized the importance of personal responsibility, saying that individuals should be free to pursue happiness without being demonized or penalized for their financial success. Beck's speech was peppered with personal anecdotes and humor, leaving the audience in stitches.
