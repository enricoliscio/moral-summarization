SUMMARY: Donald Trump criticized Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for her comments on his presidential campaign, calling them "highly inappropriate" and suggesting she should apologize and step down from her position. Ginsburg had expressed fear about a Trump presidency and joked about moving to New Zealand. Trump's response highlights the unusual nature of a sitting Supreme Court Justice publicly commenting on a political campaign. Meanwhile, Trump discussed his vice-presidential selection process, indicating that he would be announcing his choice by Friday. He mentioned several candidates, including Governor Chris Christie, Newt Gingrich, Mike Pence, Joni Ernst, and Mike Flynn, praising each for their strengths. Trump emphasized that his decision would be based on his instincts and experience with the candidates.
END OF SUMMARY.
