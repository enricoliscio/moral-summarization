SUMMARY: The US House of Representatives recently passed a sweeping anti-corruption and voting rights bill that aims to uphold the democratic values of the nation. The bill, known as the For the People Act or H.R. 1, is a campaign pledge come to life, promising to clean up Washington and restore power to the American people.

The bill includes a range of provisions to increase transparency and reduce the influence of big money in politics. This includes the disclosure of dark money groups and super PAC donors, as well as a matching system for small donations to congressional and presidential campaigns. The bill also targets ethical abuses by federal officials, such as requiring the release of tax returns and limiting the use of federal funds at businesses owned by high-ranking officials.

Additionally, the bill aims to dismantle barriers to voting by designating Election Day as a federal holiday and automatically registering citizens to vote. It also restores voting rights to those who have served felony sentences, a move that has been criticized by Republicans as a power grab. Despite the bill's strong moral framing and potential benefits to American democracy, it faces an uphill battle in the Senate, where majority leader Mitch McConnell has pledged to block it.
