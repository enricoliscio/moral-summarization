SUMMARY: Texas Governor Rick Perry, in a bid to boost his poll numbers, has released a new TV ad in Iowa accusing President Obama of waging a "War on Religion." Perry, a Methodist, has long incorporated his faith into his campaign, and the new ad emphasizes his Christian beliefs and conservative social values. In the ad, Perry criticizes Obama for allowing gays to serve openly in the military while claiming that children cannot openly celebrate Christmas or pray in school. He pledges to end Obama's supposed war on religion and defend America's religious heritage. Perry's campaign has focused on gay marriage and social issues, criticizing Obama for his stance on gay rights and his omission of God in a recent speech. Perry's ad concludes by stating that faith can make America strong again.

END OF SUMMARY.
