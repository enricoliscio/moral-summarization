SUMMARY: In a new TV ad, Texas Governor Rick Perry, a Methodist, accuses President Obama of waging a "War on Religion" and pledges to defend faith in America if elected President. The ad, titled "Strong," is airing in Iowa and emphasizes Perry's Christian faith and conservative social views. Perry criticizes Obama for allowing gays to serve openly in the military while claiming that children cannot openly celebrate Christmas or pray in school. He also mentions Obama's omission of God in his Thanksgiving speech and his administration's decision to make foreign aid decisions based on gay rights. Perry positions himself as a defender of traditional American values and religious heritage, stating that faith can make America strong again. The ad reflects Perry's recent focus on appealing to evangelical voters and highlighting social issues such as gay marriage.
END OF SUMMARY.
