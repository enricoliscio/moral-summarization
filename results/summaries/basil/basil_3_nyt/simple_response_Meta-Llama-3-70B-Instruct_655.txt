Representative Bob Etheridge, a Democrat from North Carolina, apologized for physically confronting a young man who asked if he supported President Obama's agenda. The incident, caught on video, showed Etheridge grabbing the man's hand, arm, and neck, and demanding to know his identity. Etheridge expressed regret for his actions, stating that they were uncivil and unbecoming of a member of Congress. He emphasized the importance of promoting respectful public discourse, even in the face of partisan politics. The video was circulated by conservative groups, who criticized Etheridge's behavior as unacceptable. The incident has sparked controversy, with some calling for greater accountability from elected officials. The moral framing of this story highlights the importance of civility and respect in political discourse, and the need for elected officials to model these values, even in difficult or confrontational situations.
