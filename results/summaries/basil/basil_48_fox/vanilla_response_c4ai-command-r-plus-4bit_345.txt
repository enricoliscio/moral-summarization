SUMMARY: Elizabeth Lauten, communications director for Republican Congressman Stephen Fincher, criticized President Obama's daughters, Malia and Sasha, on Facebook for their clothing choices and facial expressions during the annual turkey pardoning ceremony. Lauten's comments sparked backlash, leading her to apologize for her judgmental remarks and acknowledging the hurtfulness of her words. She attributed her change of heart to prayer and reflection. The incident highlights the scrutiny faced by the first family and the potential consequences of public criticism. Neither the White House nor Congressman Fincher commented publicly on the issue.
END OF SUMMARY.
