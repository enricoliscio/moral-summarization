SUMMARY: Elizabeth Lauten, communications director for Republican Rep. Stephen Fincher of Tennessee, faced criticism and apologized for a Facebook post criticizing President Barack Obama's daughters, Malia and Sasha, during the Thanksgiving turkey pardoning ceremony. Lauten's post described their outfits as inappropriate and suggested they lacked class and respect. She also criticized the Obamas' parenting and role model status. Lauten's comments sparked backlash, leading her to apologize, acknowledging the hurtful nature of her words and pledging to learn from the incident. The incident highlights the scrutiny faced by the first family, particularly the children, and the potential consequences of public figures' social media posts. Neither the White House nor Rep. Fincher commented publicly on the issue.
END OF SUMMARY.
