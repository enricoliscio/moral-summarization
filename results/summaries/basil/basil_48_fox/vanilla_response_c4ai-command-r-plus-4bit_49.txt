SUMMARY: Elizabeth Lauten, communications director for Republican Congressman Stephen Fincher, criticized the Obama daughters' attire and behavior during the annual turkey pardoning ceremony. In a Facebook post, Lauten suggested that Malia and Sasha Obama lacked class and respect. She also criticized President Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama for not setting a good example. Lauten's comments sparked backlash, leading her to apologize and acknowledge that her words were hurtful and judgmental. She expressed regret and pledged to learn from the incident. The incident highlights the scrutiny faced by the children of public figures and the potential consequences of insensitive remarks made on social media. Neither the White House nor Congressman Fincher issued public statements regarding the matter. Lauten's apology was posted on Facebook, but the original critical post has been deleted.
END OF SUMMARY.
