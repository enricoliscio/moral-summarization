SUMMARY:

A Republican communications director, Elizabeth Lauten, sparked controversy with a Facebook post criticizing the Obama daughters, Malia and Sasha, for their behavior during President Obama's Thanksgiving address. Lauten wrote that the girls should "show a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect," implying that they were not living up to their role model status. She also took a jab at the President and First Lady, suggesting they didn't respect their positions or the nation. However, after "many hours of prayer" and reflection, Lauten apologized for her hurtful words, acknowledging that she had "judged" the girls unfairly. She pledged to "learn and grow" from the experience, recognizing the harm caused by her thoughtless comments. The incident highlights the importance of treating others with respect and kindness, especially in public forums.

END OF SUMMARY.
