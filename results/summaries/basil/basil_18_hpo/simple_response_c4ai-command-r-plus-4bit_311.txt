SUMMARY: Republican presidential candidate Rick Perry vows to fight against what he perceives as an attack on religious values and freedom in America. Perry pledges to end "Obama's war on religion," criticizing the allowance of gays and lesbians in the military while restricting children from openly celebrating Christmas or praying in school. He promises to defend America's religious heritage and blames the Obama administration for waging a war on people of faith. Perry's campaign strategy includes significant investments in television advertising and bringing influential Texans to Iowa, despite his low poll numbers. Perry's stance raises moral concerns about equality, freedom of expression, and respect for diversity, with his comments perpetuating a divisive narrative.

END OF SUMMARY.
