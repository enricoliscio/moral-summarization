SUMMARY: Republican presidential candidate Rick Perry vows to end "Obama's war on religion" in a new TV ad, criticizing the allowance of gays in the military while restricting religious expression in schools. Perry emphasizes his Christian faith and pledges to fight against "liberal attacks" on America's religious heritage. He promises to end the "war" and allow students to openly celebrate Christmas and pray in school. Perry condemns the Obama administration's use of foreign aid to promote gay rights abroad, calling it an "endorsement of those lifestyles." Perry's campaign strategy includes significant ad spending and bringing Texans to Iowa, despite low poll numbers. He frames the issue as a moral battle, invoking faith and portraying himself as a defender against "attacks" on religious values. Perry asserts that faith can make America strong again, appealing to conservative voters' sense of moral righteousness.
END OF SUMMARY.
