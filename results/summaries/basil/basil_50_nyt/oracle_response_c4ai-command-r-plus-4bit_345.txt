SUMMARY: Susan Rice, President Obama's national security advisor, criticized Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu for his plans to address Congress regarding a nuclear deal with Iran. Rice stated that Netanyahu's actions have injected partisanship and hurt the US-Israeli relationship. The speech, arranged without the White House's knowledge, has sparked controversy and anonymous sniping. Top administration officials, including Vice President Biden and Secretary of State Kerry, have expressed their displeasure and will not attend the speech. Netanyahu refused a private meeting with Democratic senators, citing concerns of partisanship. Rice emphasized the importance of an unquestionably strong and immutable relationship between the US and Israel, but acknowledged the current damage to the relationship.
END OF SUMMARY.
