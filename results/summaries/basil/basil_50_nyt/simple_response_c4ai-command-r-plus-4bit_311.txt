SUMMARY: Susan Rice, President Obama's national security advisor, rebuked Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu for his plans to address Congress regarding a potential nuclear deal with Iran. Rice argued that Netanyahu's actions have introduced partisanship and harmed US-Israeli relations. The controversy has sparked anonymous sniping and finger-pointing, with the White House calling it a breach of protocol. Obama has stated he will not meet with Netanyahu during his visit. Vice President Biden and Secretary of State Kerry will also be absent during Netanyahu's speech. Netanyahu defended his trip, citing the need to prevent a nuclear agreement with Iran. Rice emphasized the importance of an unquestionably strong US-Israeli relationship, transcending political seasons and parties. She pledged to work to maintain this relationship despite the current tensions.

END OF SUMMARY.
