SUMMARY: Susan E. Rice, President Obama's national security advisor, criticized Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu for his plans to address Congress regarding a potential nuclear deal with Iran. Rice argued that Netanyahu's actions have introduced partisanship and harmed US-Israeli relations. The speech, arranged without the White House's knowledge, has sparked controversy and anonymous sniping between the two leaders. Top administration officials, including Vice President Biden and Secretary of State Kerry, have expressed their displeasure and hinted at the damage to the relationship. Netanyahu defended his trip, stating his obligation to prevent a nuclear agreement with Iran. Rice emphasized the importance of an unquestionably strong and apolitical relationship between the two countries and pledged to work to maintain it. This episode highlights the tense dynamic between Obama and Netanyahu, with the former focusing on maintaining diplomatic protocol and the latter prioritizing what he perceives as his country's security.
END OF SUMMARY.
