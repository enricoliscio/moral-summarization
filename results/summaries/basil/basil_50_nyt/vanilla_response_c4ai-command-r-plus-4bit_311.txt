SUMMARY: President Obama's national security adviser, Susan E. Rice, criticized Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu for his plans to address Congress about Iran's nuclear deal. Rice said that Netanyahu's actions had hurt US-Israeli relations and injected partisanship. The speech was arranged without consulting the White House, and Obama has said he will not meet with Netanyahu during his visit. Vice President Biden and Secretary of State Kerry also have conflicting schedules. Netanyahu has refused a private meeting with Democratic senators, saying it could be seen as partisan. Rice wants the US-Israel relationship to be strong and immutable, regardless of political parties in power. This episode highlights the tense relationship between Obama and Netanyahu and the damage it has caused to their countries' relations.
END OF SUMMARY.
