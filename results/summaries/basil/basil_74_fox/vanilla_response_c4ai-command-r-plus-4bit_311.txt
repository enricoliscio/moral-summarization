SUMMARY: Kathy Griffin and photographer Tyler Shields faced backlash for a photo in which Griffin holds a bloodied mask of President Trump. Griffin initially joked about the shoot, but later apologized, admitting she "went too far." She took down the image and acknowledged that it was disturbing and offensive. People, including Chelsea Clinton, expressed their disdain on Twitter. Shields defended the image as art meant to provoke discussion, but acknowledged that it would make some viewers uncomfortable. He has faced criticism in the past for controversial images, such as a photo that appeared to make light of domestic abuse. Despite the backlash, Shields stands by the collaboration with Griffin, citing freedom of speech. He also noted that the image was meant to be provocative but not taken literally.
END OF SUMMARY.
