SUMMARY: Kathy Griffin and photographer Tyler Shields sparked controversy with a photo depicting Griffin holding a bloodied mask of President Trump. Griffin initially joked about the shoot, but later apologized, acknowledging that the image was disturbing and offensive. She faced widespread backlash, including criticism from Chelsea Clinton, who called the photo "vile and wrong." Shields defended their collaboration as a form of art and freedom of speech, but the image has been widely condemned. Shields has faced similar controversies in the past for his provocative images, such as a photo depicting domestic abuse. Griffin's reps have not commented further.
END OF SUMMARY.
