SUMMARY: Kathy Griffin and photographer Tyler Shields faced backlash for a photo depicting Griffin holding a bloodied mask of President Trump. Griffin later apologized on Twitter, acknowledging that the image was disturbing and offensive. She stated that she went too far and would take down the image. People, including Chelsea Clinton, expressed their disdain for the photo on Twitter. Shields defended the image as a form of art and freedom of speech, comparing it to a movie scene. He has faced similar controversies in the past for his provocative images. Griffin's representatives did not provide additional comments.
END OF SUMMARY.
