SUMMARY: Kathy Griffin and photographer Tyler Shields faced backlash after releasing a controversial photo of Griffin holding a bloodied mask of President Trump. Griffin admitted that the image "went too far" and was "too disturbing," apologizing for offending people. She also stated that she would take down the image. People, including Chelsea Clinton, expressed their disdain for the photo shoot on Twitter. Shields defended the image as art, stating that it was meant to be provocative and make a bold political statement. He acknowledged that it would make some viewers uncomfortable but emphasized that it was not meant to be taken literally. Shields has a history of creating provocative images, including a previous photo that received criticism for appearing to make light of domestic abuse. Despite the backlash, he stands by the image as a form of artistic expression protected by freedom of speech.
