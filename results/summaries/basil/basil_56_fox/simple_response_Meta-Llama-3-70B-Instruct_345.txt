SUMMARY:

Senator Marco Rubio delivered a major foreign policy speech, emphasizing the need for a strong military and aggressive steps to combat nations that undermine global commerce. He criticized the Obama administration's cautious responses to crises, accusing them of eroding America's moral leadership. Rubio vowed to prioritize military funding, even in times of peace, to restore American strength. He also warned that nations like China and Iran will face consequences if they attempt to undermine the US's economic interests. Rubio's speech was marked by a hawkish tone, with a focus on American exceptionalism and a willingness to take decisive action. He also took aim at Democratic rivals, including Hillary Clinton, accusing them of being "loyal deputies" of the Obama administration's failed foreign policy. Rubio's stance on Cuba and Iran was uncompromising, with a strong emphasis on maintaining the embargo and distrusting Iran's nuclear program.
