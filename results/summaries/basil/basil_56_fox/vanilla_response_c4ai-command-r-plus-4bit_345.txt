SUMMARY: In his first major foreign policy speech since launching his presidential campaign, Senator Marco Rubio outlined his vision for U.S. foreign policy, emphasizing the need for a strong military and assertive leadership on the world stage. Rubio criticized the Obama administration for what he perceived as a weak and overly cautious approach to foreign policy, arguing that the U.S. is losing its global influence as a result. He pledged to prioritize military funding and take aggressive action against nations that undermine global commerce, specifically calling out Russia, China, and Iran. Rubio also took aim at former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton, blaming her for what he considers a failed foreign policy during her tenure. Rubio defended his stance on maintaining the embargo on Cuba and expressed distrust of Iran's nuclear program. He concluded by stating that a strong America leads to a more peaceful world. 

END OF SUMMARY.
