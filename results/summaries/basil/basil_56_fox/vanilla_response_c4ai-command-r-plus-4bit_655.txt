SUMMARY: In his first major foreign policy speech since launching his presidential campaign, Sen. Marco Rubio outlined a strong and aggressive approach to global affairs, prioritizing a well-funded military and asserting the U.S. as a moral leader. Rubio criticized the Obama administration for its cautious response to overseas crises and its willingness to acquiesce to hostile foreign governments. He emphasized the need for American strength and its role in maintaining global peace. Rubio also discussed his views on Cuba and Iran, defending the embargo on Cuba and arguing that Iran cannot be trusted in nuclear negotiations. He characterized his approach as hawkish and interventionist, in contrast to some of his Republican colleagues who have favored more diplomatic approaches. Rubio's speech highlighted his experience on Senate committees and his access to sensitive information, positioning himself as a knowledgeable and experienced leader in foreign policy.

END OF SUMMARY.
