SUMMARY: Former Mexican President Vicente Fox and Republican nominee Donald Trump engaged in a Twitter war ahead of Trump's visit to Mexico. Fox criticized current President Enrique Pena Nieto's decision to invite Trump, stating that he is "not welcome" in Mexico due to his offensive remarks about the country. Fox called the invitation a "big mistake" and suggested that Pena Nieto would be considered a "traitor." Trump responded on Twitter, claiming that Fox had invited him after apologizing for using profanity in a previous dispute. Fox then fired back, demanding an apology from Trump and accusing him of lying. The exchange highlights the tense relationship between Trump and Mexico, with Fox serving as a vocal critic of Trump's controversial statements and policies towards the country.
