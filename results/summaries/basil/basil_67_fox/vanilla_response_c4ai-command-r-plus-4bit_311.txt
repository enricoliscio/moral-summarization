SUMMARY: Former Mexican President Vicente Fox and Republican nominee Donald Trump engaged in a Twitter war ahead of Trump's visit to Mexico. Fox expressed his disagreement with current President Enrique Pena Nieto's decision to invite Trump, stating that he is "not welcome" in Mexico due to his offensive remarks. Fox criticized Pena Nieto's decision, suggesting it was a "big mistake" and that he would be considered a "traitor." Trump responded to Fox's comments on Twitter, referencing a previous incident where Fox had apologized for using profanity in rejecting Trump's wall proposal. Fox then fired back, demanding an apology from Trump and accusing him of lying. This exchange highlights the tense relationship between Trump and Mexican political figures, with Trump's controversial statements causing friction even before his arrival in the country.
