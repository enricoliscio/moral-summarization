SUMMARY: FBI Special Agent Peter Strzok, who exchanged anti-Trump texts with former FBI official Lisa Page during the 2016 election, was fired from the bureau despite the recommendations of a career FBI official. Strzok's attorney, Aitan Goelman, called the decision "a departure from typical Bureau practice" and argued that it was made in response to political pressure, rather than a fair examination of the facts. The firing has raised concerns about political interference and the protection of free speech.

Strzok played a prominent role in the investigations into both the Clinton and Trump campaigns during the 2016 election. He was taken off of special counsel Robert Mueller's team when his texts became public, and the DOJ's inspector general expressed concern about the potential impact of bias on investigative decisions. However, Strzok's attorney argues that there is no evidence that his personal views ever affected his work. The precise justification for Strzok's firing is unclear, but it may be related to the appearance of bias, his use of personal email for FBI business, and his extramarital relationship with Page. The decision has been criticized as a victory for those seeking to harm the country and weaken democracy.
