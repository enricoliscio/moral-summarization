SUMMARY: Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a lawsuit against Colorado in the U.S. Supreme Court, arguing that the state's legalization of recreational marijuana is unconstitutional because it violates federal law, which still prohibits the drug. The plaintiffs claim that Colorado's legalization has led to an increase in marijuana trafficking across state lines, causing harm to neighboring states. Colorado's attorney general intends to defend the state's marijuana laws, stating that the lawsuit is without merit. Supporters of the lawsuit, including an anti-legalization group, argue that Colorado's legalization violates federal law and has consequences for other states. Opponents of the lawsuit, including a marijuana policy group and a Colorado congressman, argue that Nebraska and Oklahoma are overstepping their bounds and interfering with the rights of Colorado voters to enact policies that benefit their state. The outcome of this case could have significant implications for the future of state marijuana laws across the country.
