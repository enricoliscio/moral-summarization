SUMMARY:

A Republican staffer, Elizabeth Lauten, sparked controversy with a Facebook post criticizing Malia and Sasha Obama's appearance at the White House turkey pardon ceremony. Lauten's judgmental comments told the teenagers to "try showing a little class," "dress like you deserve respect," and "don't make faces" during public events. She even implied that the Obama parents were poor role models. However, after facing backlash, Lauten apologized for her hurtful words, acknowledging that she had rushed to judgment. She recognized that her words were judgmental and could have been hurtful to the young ladies, and pledged to learn and grow from the experience. Lauten's apology emphasized the importance of respecting others, especially in public positions, and the need to avoid hurtful judgments.

END OF SUMMARY.
