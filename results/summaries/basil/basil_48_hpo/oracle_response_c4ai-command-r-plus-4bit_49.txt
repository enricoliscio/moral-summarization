SUMMARY: Elizabeth Lauten, a Republican staffer, apologized for a Facebook post that criticized Malia and Sasha Obama's appearance at the annual White House turkey pardon ceremony. Lauten's original post rushed to judgment and criticized the teenagers for their attire and facial expressions, suggesting they lacked class and respect for the occasion. She advised them to "rise to the occasion" and "act like being in the White House matters to you." However, Lauten later issued an apology, acknowledging that her words were hurtful and offensive. She expressed regret for judging the two young ladies and pledged to learn and grow from the experience. Lauten's apology recognized the importance of respecting the First Family's position and the impact of her words on others.
END OF SUMMARY.
