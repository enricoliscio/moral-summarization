SUMMARY:

A Republican staffer, Elizabeth Lauten, sparked outrage with a Facebook post criticizing Malia and Sasha Obama's behavior and appearance at the White House turkey pardon ceremony. Lauten wrote that the teenage girls should "show a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect" instead of making faces during the event. Her condescending tone and judgmental words were widely criticized. However, Lauten later apologized for her post, acknowledging that she had rushed to judgment and hurt many people with her words. She expressed regret for not treating the girls with the same kindness and respect she would have wanted as a teenager herself. Lauten's apology was a recognition that her initial words were hurtful and that she had fallen short of her own moral standards. Her experience serves as a reminder of the importance of treating others with kindness and respect, especially in public forums.

END OF SUMMARY.
