SUMMARY:

A Republican staffer, Elizabeth Lauten, sparked controversy with a Facebook post criticizing Malia and Sasha Obama's appearance at the White House turkey pardon ceremony. Lauten judged the teenagers, saying they should "try showing a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect." She also advised them not to "make faces" during public events. However, after facing backlash, Lauten apologized for her hurtful words, acknowledging that she had judged the girls unfairly. She reflected on her actions, saying she would never have wanted to be judged that way as a teenager herself. Lauten expressed regret for the pain she caused, apologizing to those she offended and pledging to learn and grow from the experience. Her apology recognized the importance of respect and the harm caused by judgmental feelings.

END OF SUMMARY.
