SUMMARY: President Obama responded to the murder of four Marines in Tennessee, calling it "heartbreaking" and asking Americans not to jump to conclusions. However, Republicans, including Louisiana Gov. Bobby Jindal, were quick to blame the President for what investigators called an act of domestic terrorism. The shooting has become part of the 2016 presidential campaign, with Republicans criticizing Obama's handling of terrorism and his policies towards Islamic extremism. The FBI identified the gunman as Mohammad Youssuf Abdulazeez, a naturalized U.S. citizen, and said it was too early to speculate on his motives. The shooting has also reignited debates about gun policies at military facilities and the threat of lone-wolf incidents. Obama's remarks were brief and focused on offering condolences and asking for patience while the investigation unfolds. The threat from the Islamic State and other extremist groups is growing, with an increasing number of U.S.-based terror plots or attacks being uncovered. This shooting is similar to other incidents during Obama's tenure, and has sparked debates about how the government confronts violent extremists and the role of radicalized Muslims in domestic terrorism.
