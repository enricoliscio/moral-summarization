The Obama administration faces a tricky political and legal dilemma regarding its position on same-sex marriage, specifically in the context of the Proposition 8 case in California. The administration must decide what arguments to present in its briefs to the U.S. Supreme Court, and the president himself is expected to make the final decision. Proposition 8, a California ballot initiative banning same-sex marriage, was deemed unconstitutional discrimination by lower courts. The Obama administration's brief is not required in this case but is expected to be filed. The key question is whether the administration will advocate for a constitutional right to marry for same-sex couples, which would invalidate laws in many states. President Obama's evolving stance on same-sex marriage, from a states' rights perspective to a more expansive view at his inauguration, suggests a potential shift in the administration's position. The ultimate decision will have important symbolic implications for both supporters and opponents of same-sex marriage.
