SUMMARY: New U.S. COVID-19 cases saw a record spike on Tuesday with over 47,000 new infections, and Dr. Anthony Fauci warned the number could soon double. States like California, Texas, and Arizona are reporting record increases, with hospital beds in short supply. Dr. Fauci stressed the need for a nationwide effort to control the virus and highlighted the uncertainty of a vaccine. The surge in cases has led to renewed criticism of President Trump's handling of the pandemic, with rival Joe Biden blaming Trump for the economic damage and loss of lives. States like California, Texas, and Florida have reversed course and closed bars, while New York and other states have imposed quarantines on travelers from hotspot states. Hospitals in Houston, Texas are seeing a rapid increase in COVID-19 patients, and experts worry that upcoming Independence Day celebrations could worsen the situation.
