SUMMARY: In his annual Christmas Day address, Pope Francis shone a light on the suffering of migrants and called for an end to conflicts around the world. He offered a message of hope and kindness, urging people to recognize and help those in need. Francis prayed for those forced to emigrate due to injustice, and he specifically mentioned the plight of Syrian refugees. He called for governments and the international community to find solutions to ongoing humanitarian crises and bring peace and security to the Middle East. The pope also urged an end to conflicts in Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa, and prayed for those persecuted for their religion. In a separate message, he and other religious leaders called on South Sudanese leaders to uphold their commitment to forming a coalition government. Pope Francis concluded his address by calling on people to recognize and alleviate the suffering around them.
END OF SUMMARY.
