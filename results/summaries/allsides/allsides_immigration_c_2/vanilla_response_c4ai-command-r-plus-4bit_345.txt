SUMMARY: In his annual Christmas Day address, Pope Francis delivered a message of hope and compassion for migrants worldwide, calling attention to the injustices that force them to leave their homes in search of a better life. He prayed for an end to conflicts and for comfort for those suffering, specifically mentioning the ongoing war in Syria and the plight of children affected by war and migration. The pope also urged people to show goodwill and find solutions to humanitarian crises. He concluded his message with a call to recognize and alleviate the suffering of those in need, emphasizing kindness and compassion. In a separate message, Pope Francis, along with two other religious officials, urged leaders of South Sudan to uphold their commitment to forming a coalition government, expressing his hope to visit the country in 2020.

END OF SUMMARY.
