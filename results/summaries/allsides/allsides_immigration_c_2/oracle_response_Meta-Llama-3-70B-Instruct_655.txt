SUMMARY: In his Christmas Day message, Pope Francis shed light on the suffering of migrants worldwide, calling for kindness and hope in the face of injustices. He prayed for God to comfort those suffering and urged people to show goodwill and find solutions to ongoing humanitarian crises. The pontiff condemned the injustices that force people to emigrate, often resulting in tragic consequences, such as drowning in the Mediterranean Sea. He also called for an end to conflicts in the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa, and prayed for those persecuted on the basis of religion. Francis urged leaders to work towards peace and security, and for individuals to recognize the suffering around them and act to help those in need. He concluded by praying for God's love to soften hearts and bring tenderness to all, brightening the darkness of the world. END OF SUMMARY.
