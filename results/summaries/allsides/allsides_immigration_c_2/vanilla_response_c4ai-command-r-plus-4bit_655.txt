SUMMARY: Pope Francis delivered his annual Christmas Day address, titled "Urbi et Orbi", from the balcony of St. Peter's Basilica, focusing his message on the plight of migrants and calling for an end to the injustices that force them to leave their homes. He highlighted the suffering of migrants who cross dangerous deserts and seas in search of a better life, only to face indifference and further hardships. The Pope prayed for comfort and peace for those affected by conflicts in the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa, specifically mentioning the ongoing suffering of the Syrian people. He also called for solutions to humanitarian crises and an end to religious persecution. Francis concluded his message with a plea to recognize and help those in need, emphasizing the importance of showing kindness and good will during the Christmas season. In a separate message, the Pope, along with two other religious officials, urged leaders of South Sudan to uphold their commitment to forming a coalition government, which could pave the way for a potential papal visit to the country in 2020.
END OF SUMMARY.
