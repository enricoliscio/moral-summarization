SUMMARY: In his annual Christmas Day address, Pope Francis offered a message of hope and a call for kindness towards migrants worldwide, shining a light on the injustices they face. He prayed for an end to conflicts and for comfort for those suffering, especially children, and called on people to show goodwill and find solutions to humanitarian crises. The pope urged an end to indifference and highlighted the plight of migrants, remembering those who have drowned in the Mediterranean Sea. In a separate message, he and other religious leaders urged South Sudanese leaders to uphold their commitment to forming a coalition government, which could pave the way for his hoped-for visit to the country in 2020. Pope Francis concluded his address with a plea to recognize and alleviate the suffering of those in need, invoking Christ's love and tenderness to brighten the world's darkness.

END OF SUMMARY.
