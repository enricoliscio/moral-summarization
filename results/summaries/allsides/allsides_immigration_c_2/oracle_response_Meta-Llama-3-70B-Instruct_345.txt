SUMMARY:

In his Christmas Day address, Pope Francis shone a light on the suffering of migrants around the world, calling for kindness and hope in the face of injustices. He prayed for God to comfort those who are suffering and urged people to show good will and find solutions to ongoing humanitarian crises. The pontiff condemned the injustices that force people to emigrate, often leading to tragic consequences, such as drowning in the Mediterranean Sea. He also called for an end to conflicts in various regions, including the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa, and prayed for those who are persecuted on the basis of religion. Francis urged leaders to find solutions to allow people to live together in peace and security, and to put an end to their unspeakable sufferings. He concluded by calling on people to recognize the suffering around us and act to help those in need, softening our hearts and making them channels of love.

END OF SUMMARY.
