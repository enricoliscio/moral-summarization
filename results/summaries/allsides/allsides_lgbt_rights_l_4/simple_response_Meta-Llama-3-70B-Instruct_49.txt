The recent hanging of two men in Iran, allegedly for consensual sodomy, highlights the complexities of US foreign policy and LGBT human rights. While the Obama administration has been pursuing a policy of constructive engagement with Iran, the execution of two gay men may complicate this effort. Conservatives, who typically oppose LGBT equality, may use this incident to criticize the administration's approach, while liberals may be hesitant to speak out due to concerns about cultural imperialism and the prioritization of strategic interests over human rights. The phenomenon of "homonationalism," where LGBT equality is invoked to justify US foreign policy, is problematic, as it can perpetuate Western conceptions of sexuality and gender identity, stifling indigenous traditions. Given these complexities, it is unlikely that the Obama administration or the LGBT movement will strongly condemn this incident, despite its tragic nature.
