SUMMARY:

The recent hanging of two men in Iran, allegedly for consensual sodomy, highlights the complexities of US involvement in LGBT human rights issues. While the Obama administration has been pursuing a policy of constructive engagement with Iran, the execution of these men may complicate this effort. Conservatives, who have historically opposed LGBT equality, are likely to use this incident to criticize the administration's approach, while progressives may be hesitant to speak out due to concerns about cultural imperialism and the imposition of Western values on non-Western cultures. The phenomenon of "homonationalism," where LGBT rights are invoked to justify American foreign policies, raises questions about the motivations behind such advocacy. Ultimately, the administration and the LGBT movement may be cautious in their response, recognizing that vocal criticism could exacerbate the situation.
