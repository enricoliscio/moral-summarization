SUMMARY: Trump's grandiosity and misuse of the term "treason" reflect a dangerous confusion between loyalty to the country and loyalty to himself, harking back to the old English idea of betrayal of the monarch. Trump's inaccurate and casual use of the term, which carries the death penalty, is disturbing and unprecedented for a US president. His references to treason, such as calling Democrats' opposition to his border measures "treasonous," show a disregard for the term's true meaning and its serious legal consequences. Trump's statements echo monarchs of the past who used treason to execute political opponents, highlighting the importance of the limited definition in the US Constitution. Trump's rhetoric blurs the line between criticism and betrayal, posing a threat to free speech and political dissent. His words are not only misleading but also potentially harmful to the democratic values and principles that uphold the nation.
