SUMMARY: In a recent shooting at the West Freeway Church of Christ in Texas, a gunman opened fire on parishioners, but a good guy with a gun, 71-year-old Jack Wilson, stopped the potential mass murderer in mere seconds. This incident highlights the ongoing debate about gun control and the right to self-defense. Texas governor Greg Abbott previously signed a bill allowing Texans with concealed-carry licenses to bring their weapons into places of worship, a move that generated hostility among gun-control advocates. While there is no easy solution to the problem of deranged people committing mass shootings, the argument that guns rarely stop these events is misleading when considering that most high-profile mass shootings happen in gun-free zones. Allowing trained and licensed citizens to carry concealed firearms can provide protection and potentially mitigate the damage inflicted by murderers. The presence of good guys with guns may also deter potential shooters, who prefer soft targets. The right to self-defense is a fundamental principle, and the ability to protect oneself and others in places of worship or schools is essential for many. However, some states have draconian laws that make it challenging for citizens to exercise this right. The summary concludes with a reflection on the importance of self-defense and the right to bear arms in the context of Jewish history and the state of Israel.
