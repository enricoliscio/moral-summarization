SUMMARY: John Lewis, a fearless leader and hero of the U.S. civil rights movement, passed away at the age of 80. Lewis is remembered for his unwavering commitment to fighting racial discrimination and his pivotal role in the Selma march, which led to the passage of the federal Voting Rights Act. On March 7, 1965, Lewis and fellow activists faced a brutal confrontation with Alabama state troopers on the Edmund Pettus Bridge, an event known as Bloody Sunday. Despite the violence and threats, Lewis stood strong, putting himself in harm's way to fight for freedom and equality. Lewis's courage and determination inspired a nation and helped to shape the course of history. In his later years, he continued to encourage others to speak up and take a stand against injustice. His legacy serves as a reminder of the power of moral courage and the enduring fight for justice and equality. END OF SUMMARY.
