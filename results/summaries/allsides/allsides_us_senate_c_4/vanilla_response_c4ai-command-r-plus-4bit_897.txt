SUMMARY: Republican senators warned President Donald Trump against firing Special Counsel Robert Mueller, who is leading the investigation into Russian meddling in the 2016 U.S. election. Senators Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham stated that firing Mueller would be detrimental to Trump's presidency. Speaker Paul Ryan's spokeswoman also supported Mueller's investigation. White House lawyer Ty Cobb denied that Trump was considering firing Mueller. Trump has intensified his criticism of the FBI and Mueller's probe following the firing of former FBI deputy director Andrew McCabe. Trump's personal lawyer called for an end to the Russia investigation, while some Republicans criticized this stance. The White House legislative affairs director attributed Trump's comments to his frustration with the prolonged investigation. Senator Marco Rubio also criticized the firing of McCabe, saying he should have been allowed to finish his term. McCabe's lawyer called Trump's tweets "childish and defamatory."
END OF SUMMARY.
