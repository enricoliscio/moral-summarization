SUMMARY:

Republican senators have warned President Donald Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, who is investigating Russian meddling in the 2016 US election. Trump has renewed his Twitter attacks on the FBI and Mueller's probe, sparking concerns that he may try to dismiss Mueller. Republican Senator Jeff Flake said Trump's comments appeared to be aimed at firing Mueller, while Senator Lindsey Graham warned that such a move would mark "the beginning of the end of his presidency." The White House has denied that Trump is considering firing Mueller. Trump has accused the FBI leadership of lies, corruption, and leaking information, and has called the Russia probe a "witch hunt." Republican lawmakers have urged Trump to let Mueller do his job, with some criticizing the president's attacks on the FBI and Mueller. The US intelligence community has concluded that Russia conducted an influence campaign to sway the 2016 election in Trump's favor.

END OF SUMMARY.
