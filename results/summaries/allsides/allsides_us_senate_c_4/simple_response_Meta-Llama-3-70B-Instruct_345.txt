SUMMARY:

Republican senators warned President Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, emphasizing that he must let federal investigators do their job in probing Russian meddling in the US election. Trump's recent Twitter attacks on the FBI and Mueller's probe have raised concerns that he may be planning to dismiss Mueller. Senators Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham cautioned that firing Mueller would be a grave mistake, with Graham stating it would mark "the beginning of the end of his presidency." The White House has denied that Trump is considering firing Mueller, but the president's actions have sparked widespread criticism. Trump's attacks on the FBI and Mueller's probe have been labeled as an attempt to thwart the investigation and undermine the rule of law. Republican lawmakers have urged Trump to let the investigation proceed without interference, emphasizing that Mueller must be allowed to do his job.

END OF SUMMARY.
