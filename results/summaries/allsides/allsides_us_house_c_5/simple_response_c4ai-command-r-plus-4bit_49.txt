SUMMARY: House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff aggressively defended himself against calls from President Trump and Republicans for him to step down, citing his past comments on the Trump campaign's contacts with Russia. Schiff listed instances of the campaign's interactions with Russia, stating that he found them unacceptable and signaling his commitment to continue the committee's investigation into Trump's links to Russia and potential compromising by foreign powers. He faced criticism from Rep. Mike Conaway and other Republican members, who accused him of promoting false narratives and damaging the panel's integrity. Rep. Michael Turner likened Schiff to Joseph McCarthy for his aggressive investigations. Democrats supported Schiff, dismissing the Republican attack as an "ambush" and a "cheap-shot." Schiff's refusal to back down and his passionate defense highlight his moral stance on the issue, seeing his actions as necessary to uphold integrity and expose potential wrongdoings. 

END OF SUMMARY
