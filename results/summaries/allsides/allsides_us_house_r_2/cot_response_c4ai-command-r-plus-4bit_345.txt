SUMMARY: The U.S. House of Representatives has launched the first public hearing of Donald Trump's impeachment inquiry, marking a pivotal moment in American history. The investigation seeks to determine whether the president used his office to pressure Ukraine for personal political gain, with the core question being whether his actions constitute impeachable offenses. The public hearing, broadcast live with real-time commentary, features the testimony of seasoned diplomats William Taylor and George Kent, who recount a striking narrative of alleged presidential misconduct. The inquiry centers on Trump's phone call with Ukraine's president, Volodymyr Zelenskiy, in which he requested a "favor" involving investigations into Democrats and his political rival, Joe Biden. The hearing aims to assess if Trump abused his power by withholding military aid to Ukraine as leverage. Despite the White House's efforts to block testimony, witnesses have come forward, and their appearances have been subpoenaed. Trump has dismissed the inquiry as a "witch hunt," yet the proceedings represent a constitutional force beyond his control. The impeachment process divides Americans and lacks a galvanizing moment akin to Watergate or a national shrug like Clinton's impeachment. The situation more closely resembles the partisanship-infused impeachment of Andrew Johnson. The hearing's outcome will have significant implications for Trump's presidency and the future expectations of the commander-in-chief's conduct.
