SUMMARY: A shooting at Noblesville West Middle School in Indiana left a student and a teacher critically injured. A male student was taken into custody following the shooting. Noblesville Police Department Chief Kevin Jowitt stated that the suspect excused himself from class and returned with two handguns. A science teacher reportedly tackled the student and swatted a gun out of his hand. The teacher was injured and underwent surgery. Authorities have not released the identities of the suspect or victims. The shooting comes a week after a similar incident in Santa Fe, Texas, that left ten people dead. Politicians, including Vice President Mike Pence and Indiana Governor Eric Holcomb, expressed their condolences and prayers for those affected by the shooting. The incident has sparked discussions about school safety and the need to protect students from gun violence.
END OF SUMMARY.
