SUMMARY:

A devastating shooting incident occurred at Noblesville West Middle School in Indiana, leaving a student and a teacher critically injured. Authorities took a male student into custody after the shooting, which was reported at 9:06 a.m. ET. The victims were rushed to the hospital, and their families were notified. The suspect and victims have not been identified. Authorities believe there is no further threat to the school, but a threat was directed to Noblesville High School, where many middle school students were bused to following the incident. The FBI and the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives are investigating the incident. A resource officer was present at the time of the incident, and a science teacher reportedly tackled the student who fired shots, saving many lives. Politicians, including Vice President Mike Pence, and the Indianapolis Colts released statements offering prayers and condolences to the victims and the Noblesville community. The incident has sparked outrage and concern, with the Indiana State Teachers Association asking, "When is enough, enough?" in response to the national crisis of school violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
