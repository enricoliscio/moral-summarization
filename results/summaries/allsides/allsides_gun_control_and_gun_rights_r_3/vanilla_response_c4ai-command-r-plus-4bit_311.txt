SUMMARY: A shooting at Noblesville West Middle School in Indiana left a student and a teacher critically injured. Authorities have taken a male student into custody as the suspect. The suspect allegedly excused himself from the classroom and returned with two handguns, at which point a teacher tackled him and swatted a gun out of his hand. The teacher, identified as Jason Seaman, was shot multiple times and underwent surgery. The suspect has not been identified, and authorities are investigating the incident. This shooting comes just a week after a similar tragedy in Santa Fe, Texas, and has sparked further discussions about school safety and gun control. Noblesville's mayor and police chief addressed the media, expressing shock and offering their thoughts and prayers to the victims and their families. Indiana Governor Eric Holcomb also released a statement, mentioning that 100 state police officers were made available to assist local law enforcement.
END OF SUMMARY.
