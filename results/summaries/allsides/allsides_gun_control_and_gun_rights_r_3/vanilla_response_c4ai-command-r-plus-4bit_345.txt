SUMMARY: A shooting at Noblesville West Middle School in Indiana left a teacher and a student critically injured. A male student was taken into custody after the incident, which occurred on Friday morning. Authorities have not identified the suspect or victims but confirmed that the situation was resolved quickly. A teacher reportedly tackled the student and swatted a gun out of his hand, preventing further injuries. The teacher was shot multiple times and underwent surgery. The school does not have metal detectors, but there was a resource officer present at the time. Many politicians, including Vice President Mike Pence, expressed their thoughts and prayers for the victims and the community. The shooting comes just a week after a similar incident in Santa Fe, Texas, that left ten people dead. Noblesville, Indiana, is a small city about 20 miles from Indianapolis, and the middle school has about 1,300 students.
END OF SUMMARY.
