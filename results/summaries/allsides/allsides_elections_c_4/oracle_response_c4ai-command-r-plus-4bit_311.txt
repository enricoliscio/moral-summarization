SUMMARY: Hillary Clinton and Tim Kaine criticized Donald Trump's plans for his first 100 days in office, contrasting them with their own focus on governing. Clinton and Kaine highlighted Trump's intention to sue women who have accused him of unwanted sexual advances, portraying him as more interested in avenging personal grievances than in leading the country. Clinton emphasized the importance of accepting election results, framing Trump's refusal to concede as a threat to America's democratic traditions and the difference between "leadership and dictatorship." She also criticized Trump's disregard for constitutional protections and his desire to jail his opponent, underlining the importance of an independent judiciary. Meanwhile, Clinton showed her support for down-ballot races, campaigning for Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty, who is challenging incumbent Pat Toomey in Pennsylvania.
