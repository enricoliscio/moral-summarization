SUMMARY: Hillary Clinton and her running mate, Tim Kaine, criticized Donald Trump's plans for his first 100 days in office, specifically his intention to sue women who have accused him of sexual misconduct. Clinton contrasted Trump's focus on personal grievances with her own commitment to governing and preparing to serve the American people. Trump's comments were made during a speech at the Gettysburg battlefield, while Clinton and Kaine spoke to reporters on their campaign plane. Clinton emphasized the importance of accepting election results and respecting democratic traditions, faulting Trump for suggesting he might not concede the election. She also promoted Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty, highlighting the importance of down-ballot races for her policy agenda. Clinton and Kaine focused on Pennsylvania, a key state in the election, with Clinton emphasizing the dangers Trump poses to America's democratic traditions and his disregard for constitutional freedoms.
END OF SUMMARY.
