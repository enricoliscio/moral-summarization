SUMMARY:

Hillary Clinton drew a stark contrast between her approach to governance and Donald Trump's, highlighting his intention to sue women who have accused him of sexual misconduct. Clinton emphasized that Trump's priority is settling personal scores rather than serving the American people. In contrast, Clinton and her running mate, Tim Kaine, are focused on preparing to govern and address the nation's pressing issues. Clinton warned that Trump's disregard for constitutional freedoms and protections poses a danger to America's democratic traditions. She criticized Trump's threat to jail her over her email controversy, noting that the US has laws, courts, and an independent judiciary. Clinton also campaigned for Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty, emphasizing the importance of down-ballot races in shaping her policy agenda. Overall, Clinton presented herself as a champion of democratic values and a responsible leader, while portraying Trump as a vengeful and authoritarian figure.
