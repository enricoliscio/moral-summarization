SUMMARY: Republican Senator Lindsey Graham threatened to delay the appointment of President Obama's nominees for the CIA and Defense Department, demanding more information about the Benghazi terror attack. Graham criticized the White House's handling of the incident that killed Ambassador Christopher Stevens and three other Americans. He accused the administration of delaying, denying, and deceiving, stating that they must be held accountable for their actions during the national crisis. Graham questioned the White House's efforts to help the Americans under attack and sought information on the president's actions that night. Democrat Jack Reed called Graham's actions unprecedented and inappropriate, claiming he was using the tragedy to block nominations. Meanwhile, Republicans expressed concerns about Defense nominee Chuck Hagel's support for Israel and his stance on Iran. Oklahoma Senator James Inhofe threatened to filibuster Hagel's nomination. Graham vowed to continue his pursuit of the truth, demanding the White House provide an accounting of their response to the Benghazi attack.
END OF SUMMARY.
