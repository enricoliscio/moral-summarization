SUMMARY: One man was killed in Louisville after police and the National Guard opened fire on a group gathered in a parking lot, further inflaming tensions in the city already grappling with protests against the unjust killing of Breonna Taylor and George Floyd. The police chief, Steve Conrad, stated that the crowd fired at officers first, resulting in both the National Guard and Louisville police returning fire, killing one person. The incident underscores the deep mistrust between law enforcement and the community, with Conrad acknowledging the need to work on this issue over the long term. The governor has authorized an independent investigation into the fatal shooting, recognizing the seriousness of the situation. This tragic killing adds to the calls for justice and underscores the urgency of addressing systemic racism and police brutality, as protesters demand accountability for the deaths of Taylor and Floyd, who died at the hands of those sworn to protect them. The public awaits further details of the incident and the release of video footage. 

END OF SUMMARY.
