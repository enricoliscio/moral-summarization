SUMMARY:

A man was shot and killed in Louisville when police and the National Guard opened fire on a crowd in a parking lot. The incident occurred after a night of destructive protests over the fatal police shootings of Breonna Taylor and George Floyd. According to Metro Police Chief Steve Conrad, the crowd fired at officers and soldiers, prompting them to return fire. The victim has not been identified. An investigation into the fatal shooting has been authorized by Gov. Andy Beshear. Police are interviewing witnesses and collecting video footage, which will be released to the public soon. The incident is likely to escalate tensions in Louisville, where protesters have been demanding justice for Taylor, an unarmed African American EMT who was killed by police in her apartment on March 13.

END OF SUMMARY.
