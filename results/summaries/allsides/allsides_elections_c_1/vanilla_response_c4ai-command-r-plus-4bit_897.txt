SUMMARY: Despite numerous predictions of his downfall, Donald Trump's presidency has proven resilient. Critics have repeatedly claimed that various scandals, crises, and policy decisions would be the end of his presidency, yet Trump has maintained relatively stable approval ratings and defied expectations. The article challenges the notion that Trump is finished, arguing that his critics' hyperbole and exaggerated narratives work in his favor. It highlights how Trump's durability is often underestimated, and how his opponents' constant declarations of his demise have consistently proven wrong. The piece also points out the similarities between Trump's approval ratings and those of previous two-term presidents, suggesting that his chances of reelection may be stronger than his critics believe. The summary concludes by suggesting that the media should refrain from declaring Trump's presidency over until after the 2020 election.
