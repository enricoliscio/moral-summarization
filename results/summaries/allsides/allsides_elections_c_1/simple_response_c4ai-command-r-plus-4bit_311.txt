SUMMARY: Time and again, pundits have predicted the end of Donald Trump's presidency, citing various issues such as the COVID-19 crisis, economic downturn, and the recent George Floyd protests. However, Trump has consistently defied these predictions, maintaining relatively high approval ratings and even surpassing his predecessors in some areas. The hyperbole and exaggerated narratives of Trump's critics often backfire, with their extreme positions only serving to strengthen Trump's position. The article highlights the disconnect between the predictions of Trump's demise and the reality of his durable support, suggesting that the "Trump is finished" narrative is premature and may even help ensure his reelection.
