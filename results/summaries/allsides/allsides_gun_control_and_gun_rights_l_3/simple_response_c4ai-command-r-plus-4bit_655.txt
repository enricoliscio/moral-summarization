SUMMARY: New details have emerged about the tragic mass shooting at the First Baptist Church in Sutherland Springs, Texas, which took the lives of 26 innocent people, including children and elderly individuals. The shooter, Devin Patrick Kelley, a 26-year-old white man with a history of domestic violence, should never have been able to purchase the assault rifle he used in the massacre. The Air Force's failure to report Kelley's court-martial for domestic abuse allowed him to slip through the cracks and gain access to a weapon that facilitated his heinous act. The motive behind the shooting appears to be a domestic dispute with his mother-in-law, who was a member of the church but was absent during the shooting. Despite the apparent absence of racial or religious motivation, the shooting remains a senseless and immoral act that has devastated the community and the nation. The lack of stricter gun control laws has once again resulted in preventable loss of life, as evidenced by the arrest of activists from Gays Against Guns during a protest demanding much-needed reform.
