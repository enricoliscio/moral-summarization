SUMMARY: President Donald Trump sparked outrage with racist and xenophobic tweets, telling four minority Democratic congresswomen to "go back" to their countries of origin, despite three of them being born in the United States. Trump denied his comments were racist, claiming that if they "hate" the country, they can leave. House Democrats condemned Trump's remarks, with House Speaker Nancy Pelosi calling them "xenophobic" and stating that "our diversity is our strength and our unity is our power." The congresswomen, known as "the Squad," responded with outrage, with Rep. Ayanna Pressley tweeting "THIS is what racism looks like." International leaders, including British Prime Minister Theresa May, also criticized Trump's language. Some Republicans, including Rep. Will Hurd and Sen. Pat Toomey, spoke out against Trump's attacks, calling them "racist" and "xenophobic." The incident highlights Trump's divisive tactics and has sparked widespread condemnation. END OF SUMMARY.
