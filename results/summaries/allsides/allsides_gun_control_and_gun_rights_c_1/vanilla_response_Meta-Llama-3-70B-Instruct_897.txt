SUMMARY:

A gunman opened fire randomly while driving through Springfield, Missouri, killing five people, including a police officer, before taking his own life. The shooting began with reports of multiple calls throughout the city, with the gunman firing from his vehicle. He eventually crashed into a Kum & Go gas station and convenience store, where he walked inside and continued shooting. Officer Christopher Walsh, 32, was shot and killed while trying to rescue a victim. Two other officers were injured, one critically. The gunman, identified as 31-year-old Joaquin S. Roman, died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound. The victims also included a store employee and two customers. The motive behind the shooting remains unclear. The police chief praised the bravery of the officers, saying they showed "significant bravery and were heroic in their actions."

END OF SUMMARY.
