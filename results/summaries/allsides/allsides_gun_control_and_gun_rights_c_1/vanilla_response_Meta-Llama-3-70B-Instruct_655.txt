SUMMARY:

A gunman opened fire randomly while driving through Springfield, Missouri, killing five people, including a police officer, before taking his own life. The shooting spree began on Sunday night, with reports of multiple shootings throughout the city. The gunman, identified as 31-year-old Joaquin S. Roman, crashed his vehicle into a convenience store and continued shooting inside, killing a store employee and two customers. Officer Christopher Walsh, 32, was shot and killed while trying to rescue a victim. Another officer, Josiah Overton, was injured. The gunman's motives remain unclear. The police chief praised the bravery of the officers, saying they showed "significant bravery and were heroic in their actions." The community is left to grieve the loss of five lives, including a police officer who died a hero.

END OF SUMMARY.
