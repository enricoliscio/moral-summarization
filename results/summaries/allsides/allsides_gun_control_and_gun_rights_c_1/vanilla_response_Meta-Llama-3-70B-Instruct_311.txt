SUMMARY:

A gunman fired randomly from his vehicle while driving through Springfield, Missouri, before crashing into a convenience store and killing five people, including a police officer, on Sunday night. The gunman, identified as 31-year-old Joaquin S. Roman, also died from a self-inflicted gunshot. Officer Christopher Walsh, 32, was shot and killed while trying to rescue a victim. Two other officers were injured, including one who was shot. The victims included a store employee and two customers. The gunman's motives remain unclear. The police chief described the incident as a "roving active shooter" moving through the city, with multiple shooting calls reported throughout the night. The police department is still investigating and grieving the loss of one of their own.

END OF SUMMARY.
