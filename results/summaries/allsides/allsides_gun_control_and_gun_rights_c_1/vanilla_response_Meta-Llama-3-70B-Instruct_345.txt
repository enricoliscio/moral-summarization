SUMMARY:

A gunman went on a shooting rampage in Springfield, Missouri, killing five people, including a police officer, and injuring two others. The shooter, identified as 31-year-old Joaquin S. Roman, fired randomly from his vehicle multiple times before crashing into a convenience store and opening fire inside. Officer Christopher Walsh, 32, was shot and killed while trying to rescue a victim. Three citizens, including a store employee and two customers, were also killed. The gunman died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound. The motive behind the shooting remains unclear. The police chief praised the bravery of the officers, saying they showed "significant bravery and were heroic in their actions." The community is in shock and mourning, with the police department still investigating multiple crime scenes.

END OF SUMMARY.
