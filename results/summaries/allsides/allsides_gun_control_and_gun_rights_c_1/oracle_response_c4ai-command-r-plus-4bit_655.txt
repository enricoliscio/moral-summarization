SUMMARY: A gunman fired randomly from his vehicle and crashed into a convenience store in Springfield, Missouri, killing four people and injuring two others, including a police officer, before taking his own life. The shooting began as a "roving active shooter" situation, with the gunman driving through the city and firing from his vehicle before crashing into the store. Officer Christopher Walsh, 32, was killed as he tried to rescue a victim inside the store. Two other officers showed significant bravery and were heroic in their actions, according to Police Chief Paul Williams. Three other men, Troy D. Rapp, Shannon R. Perkins, and Matthew J. Hicks-Morris, were also killed in the shooting. The gunman's motives remain unclear, and the shooting has left the community in shock and sorrow. The police department is grieving the loss of one of their own and is still investigating multiple crime scenes.
END OF SUMMARY.
