SUMMARY: On Saturday, President Trump held a campaign rally in Tulsa, Oklahoma, where he focused on stoking division and fear among Americans rather than highlighting his accomplishments or plans for a second term. He downplayed the severity of the COVID-19 pandemic and mocked those who criticized his handling of the crisis. Trump's speech was filled with attacks on various groups, including journalists, athletes, Democrats, and immigrants. He used racist and xenophobic language, pitting different ethnic groups against each other and challenging the loyalty of naturalized Americans. Trump also encouraged violence against protesters and praised ICE agents for using force against immigrants. He suggested sending troops to Seattle to put down domestic unrest and expressed his eagerness to do so. The rally highlighted Trump's divisive and destructive approach to politics, appealing to hatred and strife rather than unity and prosperity.
