SUMMARY: On Saturday night in Tulsa, Oklahoma, President Trump held a campaign rally as part of his reelection bid, marking his first rally since the coronavirus outbreak. Trump's speech focused on stoking division and enmity, targeting various groups such as journalists, Democrats, athletes who kneel during the national anthem, and immigrants. He made racist remarks, referred to the coronavirus as "Kung Flu," and questioned the loyalty of naturalized Americans. Trump also encouraged violence and praised ICE agents who use force against immigrants. He dismissed the severity of the coronavirus pandemic and mocked efforts to control its spread. The rally highlighted Trump's intent to continue and escalate his divisive and destructive rhetoric in his reelection campaign.

END OF SUMMARY.
