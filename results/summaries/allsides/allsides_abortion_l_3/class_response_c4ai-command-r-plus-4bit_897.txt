SUMMARY: In March, the Trump administration issued a final rule barring clinics that provide or refer patients for abortions from receiving federal family planning funds. This "domestic gag rule" has been challenged in court by state officials and reproductive rights groups, who argue that it will leave low-income patients without access to essential healthcare services. The rule was temporarily blocked by a preliminary injunction from the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals on July 3, preventing it from taking effect while legal challenges continue.

The rule bars Planned Parenthood and other providers that perform abortions or offer abortion referrals from receiving Title X funding, which pays for birth control and other family planning services for low-income patients. Opponents of the rule, such as Planned Parenthood and the American Medical Association, argue that it is unethical and illegal, as it violates laws requiring unbiased counseling for pregnant patients. They also argue that the rule will compromise patient care and limit access to contraception and abortion.

Abortion opponents, however, applaud the rule, stating that organizations that provide abortions should not receive federal support. The Trump administration's move to restrict Title X funds is part of a broader effort to limit access to contraception and abortion and ramp up anti-abortion rhetoric. The rule was first proposed under President Reagan and has faced legal challenges since its inception.
