SUMMARY: An election-year bill designed to ensure women receive equal pay for equal work was blocked by Republicans who argued that the bill would primarily benefit trial lawyers instead of addressing the issue of pay discrimination. Democrats, including President Obama and Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid, criticized the Republican blockade, accusing them of prioritizing partisan politics over the rights of American women and families. The bill, supported by pay equity advocate Lilly Ledbetter, aimed to close loopholes in the 1963 Equal Pay Act by requiring employers to justify pay differences based on job performance rather than gender and by allowing women to sue for damages if they faced discrimination. Despite the Democratic efforts, the bill failed to pass with only 52 votes. The chief sponsor of the bill, Senator Barbara Mikulski, vowed to continue the fight for equal pay rights for women, refusing to accept defeat.
END OF SUMMARY.
