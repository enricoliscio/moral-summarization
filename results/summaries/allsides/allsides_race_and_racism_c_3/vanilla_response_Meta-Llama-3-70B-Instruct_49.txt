The article discusses the significance of George Floyd's death in sparking a nationwide movement against racial violence and police brutality. The article notes that Floyd's case is unique due to the circumstances of his death, which was slow and deliberate, unlike most police shootings. The incident was also fueled by the cumulative anger over recent controversial killings, including Ahmaud Arbery, Breonna Taylor, and Tony McDade. The article suggests that the coronavirus pandemic, which has disproportionately affected black Americans, and President Trump's incendiary tweets may have contributed to the widespread protests. The article concludes that ongoing court cases will keep the issue of police violence in the news, and that Floyd's death may be remembered as a pivotal moment of the decade.
