SUMMARY: President Trump described the Las Vegas shooting as "an act of pure evil," offering his prayers and condolences to the victims and their families. In a somber address to the nation, he emphasized the role of faith and unity in the face of this tragedy, the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history, with 58 dead and 515 hospitalized. The president called upon the bonds of faith, family, and shared values, stating that "our unity cannot be shattered by evil" and that even "in the darkest space, a single ray of hope" can provide solace. He announced plans to visit Las Vegas and meet with those affected by the tragedy, including law enforcement, first responders, and families of the victims. The president's words sought to provide comfort and strength, acknowledging the grief and broken hearts of those impacted. The attack, carried out by the suspect, Stephen Paddock, has left the nation reeling, with prayers and support pouring in from across the country. First Lady Melania Trump also expressed her condolences and prayers for those affected. The president and the first lady continue to pray for the nation and for the day when evil, hatred, and fear will no longer threaten the innocent.
END OF SUMMARY.
