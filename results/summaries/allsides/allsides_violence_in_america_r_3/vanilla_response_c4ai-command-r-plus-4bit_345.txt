SUMMARY: President Trump addressed the nation following the Las Vegas mass shooting, calling it an "act of pure evil." The attack left 58 dead and 515 injured, making it the deadliest in modern U.S. history. Trump offered prayers and solace to the victims' families and emphasized the nation's unity and shared values. He announced plans to visit Las Vegas on Wednesday to meet with law enforcement, first responders, and families. The suspect, 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, was killed, and his roommate, Marilou Danley, was located. The motive behind the attack remains unclear. First Lady Melania Trump also offered her condolences. The president's speech focused on faith and unity, with Trump seeking to comfort the nation in a "dark period." He highlighted the role of law enforcement and first responders and noted the use of automatic weapons in the attack. Trump's address aimed to provide solace and strength to a grieving nation.
