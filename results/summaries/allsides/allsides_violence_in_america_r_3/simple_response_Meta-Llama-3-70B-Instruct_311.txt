SUMMARY:

President Trump condemned the Las Vegas attack as an "act of pure evil" that left 58 people dead and 515 hospitalized. In a unifying speech, he offered prayers to the victims' families and emphasized the importance of faith, family, and shared values in the face of evil. Trump called for national unity, saying it cannot be shattered by violence, and expressed hope that even in darkness, a single ray of hope can illuminate. He also announced a visit to Las Vegas to meet with law enforcement, first responders, and victims' families. The president ordered flags to be flown at half-staff to honor the victims. The suspect, Stephen Paddock, was killed, and authorities are investigating his motive. Trump's speech emphasized the need for comfort, hope, and unity in the face of evil, citing scripture and calling for the banishment of evil.

END OF SUMMARY.
