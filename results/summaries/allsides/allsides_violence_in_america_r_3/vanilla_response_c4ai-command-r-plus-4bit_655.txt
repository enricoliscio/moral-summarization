SUMMARY: President Trump addressed the nation following the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history, calling it an "act of pure evil." The shooting in Las Vegas left 58 people dead and 515 injured. Trump offered prayers and solace to the families of the victims and emphasized the nation's unity in the face of such tragedy. He also announced plans to visit Las Vegas to meet with law enforcement, first responders, and families of victims. The president's speech focused on faith and the bonds of unity that cannot be shattered by evil. The suspect, identified as 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, was killed by authorities, and his roommate is considered a person of interest. Officials did not immediately reveal a motive for the attack. First Lady Melania Trump also offered her condolences and prayers for Las Vegas. White House Press Secretary Sarah Sanders stated that the president has been in contact with Nevada's governor, Las Vegas' mayor, and law enforcement officials.
END OF SUMMARY.
