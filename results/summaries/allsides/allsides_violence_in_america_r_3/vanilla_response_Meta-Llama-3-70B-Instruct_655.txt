SUMMARY:

President Trump addressed the nation on Monday morning, calling the Las Vegas mass shooting an "act of pure evil" that left 58 people dead and 515 hospitalized. He announced that he will visit Las Vegas on Wednesday to meet with law enforcement, first responders, and families of victims. The president ordered flags to be flown at half-staff at the White House and other federal buildings to honor the victims. In a somber speech, Trump offered prayers and condolences to the families of the victims, emphasizing the importance of unity and faith in the face of evil. He also spoke with Nevada Governor Brian Sandoval, Las Vegas Mayor Carolyn Goodman, and Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department's Sheriff Joe Lombardo. The suspect, 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, was killed, and authorities are investigating the motive behind the attack.

END OF SUMMARY.
