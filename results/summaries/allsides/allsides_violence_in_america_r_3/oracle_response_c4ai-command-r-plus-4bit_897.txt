SUMMARY: In the wake of the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history, President Trump delivered a somber address, calling the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil." The shooting left 58 people dead, 515 hospitalized, and countless others traumatized. The president offered his prayers and condolences to the victims' families, invoking faith and unity in the face of tragedy. He emphasized that our bonds of faith, family, and shared values "cannot be broken by violence." The president, along with the first lady, Melania Trump, prayed for an end to evil and for the innocent to be safe from hatred and fear. The suspect, 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, was killed, and his roommate, Marilou Danley, was located. Law enforcement and first responders demonstrated bravery and resilience, with some officers among the victims. Officials are working to uncover the motive behind this well-planned and devastating attack. The president's address highlighted the nation's grief and his administration's support for those affected by this horrific act of violence.
END OF SUMMARY.
