SUMMARY: In the wake of the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history, President Trump addressed the nation, calling the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil." The shooting left 58 dead and over 500 injured. Trump offered prayers and solace to the victims' families, emphasizing the nation's unity and resilience in the face of such tragedy. He highlighted the bonds of faith, family, and shared values that unite Americans and asserted that "our unity cannot be shattered by evil." The president also announced plans to visit Las Vegas to meet with those affected by the tragedy. Trump's speech was unifying and faith-focused, with references to scripture and a call for comfort and hope. He expressed his condolences and sympathies, with First Lady Melania Trump also offering her prayers and support on Twitter. The suspected shooter, 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, was killed, and authorities are working to determine a motive. The attack has left the nation reeling, with law enforcement and leaders seeking answers to prevent future tragedies.
