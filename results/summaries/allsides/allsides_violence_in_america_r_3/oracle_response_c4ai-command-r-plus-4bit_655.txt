SUMMARY: In the wake of the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history, President Trump denounced the Las Vegas attack as an "act of pure evil." 58 innocent people were killed and 515 were hospitalized due to this heinous act of violence. The president, in a somber and unifying address, offered his prayers and condolences to the families of the victims, invoking faith and shared values to comfort the nation during this "dark period." He affirmed that "our unity cannot be shattered by evil" and that even "in the darkest space, a single ray of hope can illuminate." The suspect, 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, was killed, and his roommate, Marilou Danley, was located. Law enforcement and first responders demonstrated bravery and resilience in the face of this tragedy. The president and the first lady, Melania Trump, expressed their deepest sympathies and prayers for the victims and their families, seeking solace in their faith. The nation grieves alongside the families, with heavy hearts and a steadfast resolve to support one another in the face of such senseless evil.

END OF SUMMARY.
