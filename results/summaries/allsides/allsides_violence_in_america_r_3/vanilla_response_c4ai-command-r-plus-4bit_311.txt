SUMMARY: In the wake of the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history, President Trump addressed the nation, calling the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil." The shooting left 58 dead and 515 hospitalized. Trump offered prayers and solace to the victims' families and emphasized the nation's unity in the face of such tragedies. He also announced plans to visit Las Vegas and met with law enforcement, first responders, and families of the victims. The president, along with the first lady, Melania Trump, expressed their condolences and prayers for the entire nation. Authorities identified the suspect as 64-year-old Stephen Paddock and are working to determine a motive for the well-planned attack. The president's address struck a somber and unifying tone as he sought to comfort a nation reeling from the tragic event.
END OF SUMMARY.
