SUMMARY: President Trump described the Las Vegas attack as an "act of pure evil," offering his condolences and prayers to the victims and their families. In a somber address, he referred to scripture and emphasized the importance of faith, family, and shared values in unifying the nation during this difficult time. The president acknowledged the deadly nature of the shooting, which left 58 people dead and over 500 hospitalized, making it the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history. He also announced his plans to visit Las Vegas and meet with those affected by the tragedy, including law enforcement, first responders, and families of the victims. The suspected attacker, 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, was killed, and his roommate was located by authorities. The president, along with the first lady, Melania Trump, who also offered her condolences, prayed for a day when evil would be banished and the innocent would be safe from hatred and fear. Trump's words sought to bring comfort and highlight the resilience of the American spirit in the face of such a tragic and senseless act of violence.
END OF SUMMARY.
