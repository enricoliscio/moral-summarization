SUMMARY: President Trump described the Las Vegas attack as "an act of pure evil," offering his condolences and prayers to the victims and their families. The mass shooting, now considered the deadliest in modern U.S. history, left 58 people dead and 515 hospitalized. In a unifying and faith-focused speech, Trump emphasized the nation's unity and called upon shared values of faith and family. He sought solace in scripture and expressed hope, even in the face of darkness and violence. The president announced plans to visit Las Vegas to meet with those affected and ordered flags to be flown at half-staff at the White House and other federal buildings to honor the deceased. The suspect, identified as 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, was killed, and authorities are working to uncover a motive for this well-planned and devastating attack. First Lady Melania Trump also offered her prayers and condolences, expressing her heartfelt sympathy for those impacted by this tragic event.
END OF SUMMARY.
