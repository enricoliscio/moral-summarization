SUMMARY: President Trump called the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil," offering prayers and support to the victims and their families. He emphasized the nation's unity and resilience in the face of such tragedies, drawing on faith and shared values as sources of strength. The president recognized the heroism of law enforcement and first responders, and he announced plans to visit Las Vegas to meet with those affected by the tragedy. He also ordered flags to be flown at half-staff, honoring those who lost their lives in the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history. The suspect, identified as 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, carried out a well-planned and coordinated attack, causing devastation and heartbreak. The president, joined by First Lady Melania Trump, expressed their deepest condolences and prayers for the nation, seeking solace and hope amidst the darkness. The White House Press Secretary confirmed the president's communication with Nevada officials and law enforcement, demonstrating a commitment to addressing this horrific act of violence.
