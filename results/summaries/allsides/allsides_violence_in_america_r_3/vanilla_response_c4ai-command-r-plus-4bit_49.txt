SUMMARY: President Trump addressed the nation following the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history in Las Vegas, calling the attack an "act of pure evil." The shooting left 58 dead and 515 hospitalized. Trump offered his prayers and condolences to the victims and their families, emphasizing that "God lives in the hearts of those who grieve." He also announced plans to visit Las Vegas on Wednesday to meet with law enforcement, first responders, and families of victims. The president highlighted the nation's unity and called upon shared values of faith and family to heal during this difficult time. The suspect, identified as 64-year-old Stephen Paddock, was killed by authorities, and his roommate, Marilou Danley, is considered a person of interest. Officials are still investigating the motive behind the attack. First Lady Melania Trump also offered her condolences and prayers to those affected by the tragedy.
END OF SUMMARY.
