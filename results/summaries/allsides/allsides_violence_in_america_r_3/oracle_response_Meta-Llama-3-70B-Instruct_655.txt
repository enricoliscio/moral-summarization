SUMMARY: President Trump addressed the nation, calling the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil" that left 58 people dead and 515 hospitalized. He announced a visit to Las Vegas to meet with law enforcement, first responders, and families of victims, and ordered flags to be flown at half-staff to honor those who died. In a unifying speech, Trump offered prayers and condolences to the families of victims, saying "our unity cannot be shattered by evil" and "we pray for the day when evil is banished and the innocent are safe from hatred and from fear." The suspect, Stephen Paddock, was killed, and authorities are investigating the motive behind the "well-planned" attack. Trump emphasized the importance of faith, family, and shared values in the face of "chaos" and "violence." The First Lady also offered her condolences, saying "my heart and prayers go out to victims, families & loved ones!" END OF SUMMARY.
