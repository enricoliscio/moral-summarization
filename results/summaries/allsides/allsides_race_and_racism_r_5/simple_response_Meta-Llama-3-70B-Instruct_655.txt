SUMMARY:

President Obama urged Americans to remain optimistic about race relations despite recent tragic events, emphasizing that the actions of a few individuals should not define the entire nation. He acknowledged the pain and anger caused by the fatal shootings of black men by police officers and the subsequent killing of five law-enforcement officials in Dallas. However, he insisted that race relations have not regressed to the 1960s and that the country has made progress. Obama also renewed his commitment to stopping gun violence, citing the need to make it harder for people to carry out their anger with weapons. He sympathized with police officers who have to make difficult decisions in communities where guns are prevalent. The president emphasized that violent crime is down in the US and urged calm, while also acknowledging the need to confront terrorist threats and partner with countries to eliminate them.

END OF SUMMARY.
