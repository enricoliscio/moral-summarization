SUMMARY: President Obama addressed Americans about race relations and recent tragic events in Dallas, where five law enforcement officials were killed by a gunman. Obama urged optimism and insisted that race relations have not reached a new low. He highlighted the decrease in violent crime over the years and his commitment to stopping gun violence. Obama also revived a task force to discuss next steps with police officers, community, and civil rights activists. The President compared the Dallas sniper to the Orlando shooter, calling them "demented individuals" who do not represent Americans. He declined to label the Dallas attack as a hate crime but emphasized the difficulty of interpreting the shooter's motives. Obama acknowledged the challenges faced by police officers in gun-filled communities and the need to address the gun issue. He also addressed Brexit, ISIS, and NATO, emphasizing the U.S.'s continued partnership with Europe. Obama reflected on his two terms as President, acknowledging the ongoing challenge of confronting non-state actors like ISIS. He emphasized the importance of partnering with countries to eliminate terrorist threats.
END OF SUMMARY.
