SUMMARY: President Obama addressed the recent tragic events in Dallas, where five police officers were killed by a gunman, and the deaths of two black males at the hands of police officers. He urged Americans to remain optimistic about race relations and insisted that the situation has not reached a new low. Obama also highlighted his commitment to stopping gun violence and discussed the complexities of police work in communities with high gun ownership. He announced the revival of a task force to discuss next steps in improving race relations and acknowledged the challenges of combating "non-state actors" like the Islamic State group. Obama commented on Brexit, emphasizing the importance of a sensible and orderly exit process. He reflected on his two terms as president, noting the shift from active combat to training and assisting partner nations' forces. The president also discussed the permanence of globalization and its potential impact on inequality.
END OF SUMMARY.
