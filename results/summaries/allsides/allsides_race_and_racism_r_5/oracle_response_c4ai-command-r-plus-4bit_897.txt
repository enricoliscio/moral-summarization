SUMMARY: President Obama addressed Americans about race relations and gun violence in the wake of recent tragic events, urging optimism and unity. He insisted that race relations have not reached a new low and committed to reviving a task force to address these issues. Obama emphasized the need to make it harder for people to act on their anger with weapons and drew parallels between the Dallas sniper and the Orlando shooter, calling them "demented individuals" who don't represent Americans. He declined to label the Dallas attack a hate crime but acknowledged the challenges faced by police in gun-filled communities. The President also discussed Brexit, ISIS, and NATO, emphasizing the continued partnership between the US and Europe. He reflected on his time in office, acknowledging the ongoing threat of non-state actors like ISIS and the need for global partnerships to eliminate terrorist threats. Obama also addressed the dangers of inequality stemming from globalization.

END OF SUMMARY.
