SUMMARY: President Obama spoke to Americans about race relations in the wake of recent fatal shootings of black males by police and the killing of five Dallas law enforcement officers. He urged optimism and denied that race relations have reached a new low. Obama announced the revival of a task force formed after the Ferguson shooting to discuss next steps with police officers, community activists, and others. He also renewed his commitment to stopping gun violence and addressing the issue of "demented individuals" carrying out attacks. Obama addressed the challenges faced by police in gun-filled communities and called for sensible gun control measures. Reflecting on his presidency, he noted the shift from active combat to training and assisting partner nations, with the exception of direct attacks against the Islamic State group. He commented on Brexit, urging a sensible and orderly exit process. Obama acknowledged the challenges of globalization and inequality but stated it is here to stay.
END OF SUMMARY.
