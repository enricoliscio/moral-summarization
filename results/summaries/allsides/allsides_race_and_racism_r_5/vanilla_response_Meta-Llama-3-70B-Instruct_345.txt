SUMMARY:

President Obama addressed the recent police shootings and retaliatory attack in Dallas, urging Americans to remain optimistic about race relations. He emphasized that the actions of a few individuals should not define the entire nation. Obama also revived a task force formed after the Ferguson shooting to discuss next steps with police officers, community activists, and civil rights advocates. He reiterated his commitment to stopping gun violence and noted that violent crime in the US has decreased over the past 15 years. The president sympathized with police officers working in gun-filled communities and stressed the need to address the gun issue. He also attempted to calm public anxiety about personal safety, citing declining violent crime rates. Additionally, Obama discussed his goals for partnering with countries to eliminate terrorist threats and addressed the Brexit situation, emphasizing the importance of a sensible and orderly exit process.

END OF SUMMARY.
