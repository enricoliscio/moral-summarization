SUMMARY: President Obama addressed Americans about race relations and gun violence in the wake of recent tragic events, urging optimism and highlighting improvements in violent crime rates. He emphasized the need to make it harder for people to act on their anger with weapons and committed to stopping gun violence. Obama compared the Dallas sniper and the Orlando shooter as "demented individuals" who do not represent Americans. He acknowledged the challenges faced by police officers in communities with high gun prevalence and advocated for addressing the gun issue to ensure their safety. The president also discussed Brexit, ISIS, and NATO, expressing continued support for Europe and acknowledging the ongoing threat of "non-state actors" like ISIS. Obama reflected on his time in office, noting the shift from active combat to training and assisting partner nations, and acknowledged the persistence of global inequality.
END OF SUMMARY.
