SUMMARY: The article discusses the act of forgiveness shown by the victims of the Charleston church shooting towards the perpetrator, Dylann Roof, and how it showcased the power of Christian love and nobility. The author admires the strength and grace of the victims but also expresses a sense of frustration and irritation. They question if it is less admirable to respond with righteous rage and if it is more commendable to respond to an act of violence with passivity and acceptance. The author explores the different approaches taken by African-Americans in response to their suffering and oppression, either through Christian forgiveness or through nationalism and political violence. They question why, given the history of racial injustice and abuse suffered by African-Americans, there isn't more outward violence directed towards white America. The article concludes by expressing gratitude that the victims of these injustices have chosen a less confrontational response.
