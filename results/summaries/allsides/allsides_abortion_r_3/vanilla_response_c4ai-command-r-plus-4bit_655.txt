SUMMARY: In response to recent state-level laws restricting abortion access, Democratic presidential candidates are taking steps to showcase their commitment to protecting reproductive rights. Cory Booker has proposed creating a "White House Office of Reproductive Freedom" and released a plan to "advance reproductive rights," including executive and legislative actions. Kristen Gillibrand argues that laws restricting abortion are "against Christian faith," emphasizing the separation of church and state. She, along with other candidates, supports enshrining abortion access into federal law. Joe Biden's campaign has also expressed support for this idea. Democratic lawmakers are reintroducing the "Women's Health Protection Act" to safeguard abortion rights and Roe v. Wade. Senator Richard Blumenthal emphasizes the urgency of the situation, stating that women's reproductive rights are under significant threat. These efforts reflect the Democratic Party's strong stance on maintaining and expanding abortion access across the United States.

END OF SUMMARY.
