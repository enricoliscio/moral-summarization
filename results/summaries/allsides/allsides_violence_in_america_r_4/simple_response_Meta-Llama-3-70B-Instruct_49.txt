SUMMARY:

A protest in Minnesota turned violent, resulting in 102 arrests and 21 police officers injured. The demonstration, sparked by recent police killings of black men, began peacefully but escalated into rioting with protesters throwing rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices at police officers. Authorities used smoke bombs to clear the area, and police officers were left with significant injuries. The incident was condemned by the families of the victims, who also denounced the attack on Dallas police officers. The police chief expressed disgust at the violence, stating that it was the first time in his 28 years of service that he had seen such violence towards public servants. The event highlights the need for peaceful protests and respect for law enforcement, while also acknowledging the underlying issues of racial tensions and police brutality that sparked the demonstrations.

END OF SUMMARY.
