SUMMARY:

The article argues that law enforcement is essential to maintain civilization and that fear of punishment is a key factor in making citizens comply with laws. The absence of deterrence leads to a breakdown in law and order, as seen in the case of the migrant caravan and campus protests. The article criticizes the lack of consequences for violators, citing examples of celebrities who have made threats against the president and protesters who disrupt public events without facing legal ramifications. It warns that ignoring laws can become contagious and boomerang, leading to anarchy and the erosion of freedom. The article concludes that without effective law enforcement, even the most well-intentioned laws and moral principles are meaningless.

END OF SUMMARY.
