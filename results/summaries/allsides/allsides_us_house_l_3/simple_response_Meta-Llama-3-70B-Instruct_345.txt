SUMMARY:

The controversy surrounding Rep. Ilhan Omar's comments on Israel has sparked a heated debate. While Omar's defenders argue that her criticism of US-Israel policy is being smeared as anti-Semitic, others believe that her language has played into harmful tropes about dual loyalty. The author argues that even if Omar's intentions are pure, her words have value on their own terms because they are the right thing to do. The Democratic Party's decision to condemn dual-loyalty smears without referring to Omar by name is problematic, as it deflects attention from the Israeli government's occupation of the West Bank and its treatment of Palestinians. The author also criticizes the party's refusal to listen to activists who reject the party's stance on Israel, and instead prioritizes avoiding offense to American Jews. Ultimately, the controversy highlights the need for careful language and a nuanced approach to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.
