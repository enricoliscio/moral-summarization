SUMMARY: The author, a mayor, reflects on the devastating impact of mass shootings on communities, driven by racist ideology, malice, and hate speech. The grim circle of mayors, from Las Vegas to Parkland, must lead their people forward in the wake of unthinkable tragedy. The author emphasizes the agonizing responsibility to take action, rather than relying on NRA talking points. Mayors are empowering young people to call out hate speech and take courageous steps to keep weapons out of the wrong hands. The author shares personal connections to cities affected by gun violence, including Gilroy, Dayton, and El Paso, where their ancestors found freedom and belonging. The author's Youth Council to End Gun Violence advises on gun safety and fights against accepting a status quo that normalizes gun violence. The goal is to create a nation where everyone feels safe and belongs. END OF SUMMARY.
