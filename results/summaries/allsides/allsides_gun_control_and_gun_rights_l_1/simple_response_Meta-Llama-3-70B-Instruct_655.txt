SUMMARY:

The author, a mayor, reflects on the devastating impact of mass shootings on communities across the US. Mayors from diverse backgrounds are united in their grief and determination to take action against gun violence, driven by racist ideology, malice, and hate speech. They recognize that Washington's leadership is lacking and instead, are empowering young people to demand change. The author shares personal connections to cities affected by mass shootings, including Gilroy, Dayton, and El Paso, where their ancestors found freedom and belonging. They emphasize the importance of involving and listening to young people, who are driving the movement for gun safety and demanding a nation where everyone feels safe. The author's moral framing emphasizes the need for courageous leadership, the importance of confronting intolerance and hate speech, and the responsibility to create a safer future for all.

END OF SUMMARY.
