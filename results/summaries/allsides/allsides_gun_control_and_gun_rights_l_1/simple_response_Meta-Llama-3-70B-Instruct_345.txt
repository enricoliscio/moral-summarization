SUMMARY:

The author, a mayor, reflects on the devastating impact of mass shootings on cities and towns across the US. Mayors from diverse backgrounds are united in their determination to take action against gun violence, driven by racist ideology and hate speech. They recognize the need to empower young people, who are demanding change, to create a safer society. The author shares personal connections to cities affected by mass shootings, including Gilroy, Dayton, and El Paso, where their ancestors sought freedom and belonging. They emphasize the importance of involving young people in the conversation, citing the US Conference of Mayors Youth Summit and their own Youth Council to End Gun Violence. The author's goal is to create a nation where everyone feels safe and belongs, a legacy they want to leave for their daughter. By working together, mayors and young people can create a better future, free from fear of violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
