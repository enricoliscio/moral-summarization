SUMMARY:

The author, a mayor, reflects on the devastating impact of mass shootings in various cities and towns, including Las Vegas, Orlando, San Bernardino, Parkland, Gilroy, Dayton, and El Paso. These tragedies are often driven by racist ideology, malice towards immigrants, and hate speech. The author emphasizes that mayors from across the political spectrum are taking action to address the deadly nexus between intolerance and the obsession with guns, as they cannot rely on leadership from Washington. The author highlights the importance of empowering young people to call out hate speech and take meaningful steps to prevent guns from falling into the wrong hands. The author's personal connection to these cities, through their family's history of fleeing violence and seeking freedom, underscores the moral imperative to create a nation where everyone belongs and feels safe. The author's commitment to involving and taking seriously the concerns of young people is crucial to achieving this goal.

END OF SUMMARY.
