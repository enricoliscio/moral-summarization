SUMMARY: In the wake of mass shootings and rampant gun violence, mayors from various cities and towns are taking matters into their own hands to address the deadly combination of intolerance and gun obsession. They are focusing on empowering young people to speak out against hate speech and advocating for stronger gun control measures to keep weapons out of the wrong hands. The author, a mayor himself, reflects on the personal connection he feels to cities like Gilroy, Dayton, and El Paso, where his ancestors first discovered freedom and belonging. He emphasizes the importance of listening to and involving young people in the fight against gun violence, as they are armed only with righteous demands for a safer future. The author concludes that, despite the challenges and heartbreak, it is crucial to keep striving for a nation where everyone belongs and feels safe, leaving such an inheritance for future generations.

END OF SUMMARY.
