SUMMARY: A federal judge struck down a Trump administration rule that would have allowed more health care workers to refuse to participate in abortions or other procedures on moral or religious grounds, saying that the Health and Human Services Department overstepped its authority and went beyond existing law. The judge, Paul A. Engelmayer, also noted that the rule could be costly and damaging to emergency care, and that the rationale for it was based on untrue claims about a significant increase in complaints from workers being forced to violate their conscience. The rule was challenged by a coalition of states, local governments, and health organizations who argued that it would be discriminatory and interfere with people's access to health care. They were successful in blocking it, with the judge finding that it inhibited employers' ability to organize their workplaces efficiently and risked jeopardizing access to healthcare for vulnerable populations. The ruling highlighted the moral dilemma between protecting the rights of health care workers to act according to their beliefs and ensuring that patients have access to the care they need, with the judge ultimately deciding that the administration had acted outside its authority and in violation of existing laws.
