SUMMARY: President Obama expressed concern about allegations of Russian hacking into the U.S. election system and criticized Republicans for their positive views of Russian President Vladimir Putin. Obama stated that there is great confidence in the assessment that Russia carried out the hacking and that there will be consequences for the cyberattacks. He also criticized journalists for publishing news stories based on stolen Democratic documents. Obama blamed partisan divides in Washington and expressed hope that President-elect Trump will show concern about foreign influence in the election process. He declined to answer directly if he believed Russian meddling caused Clinton's loss but did say that she was not treated fairly by the media. The news conference was briefly interrupted when a journalist fainted. Obama offered assistance and expressed concern for the journalist's well-being.
END OF SUMMARY.
