SUMMARY: President Obama expressed concern over allegations of Russian hacking into the U.S. election system, urging President-elect Donald Trump to address the issue appropriately. Obama criticized Republicans for their positive views of Vladimir Putin and their focus on defeating Hillary Clinton rather than standing against Russian meddling. He asserted that there would be consequences and increased costs for Russia's cyberattacks. Obama also chided journalists for covering stories based on stolen Democratic documents. The president emphasized the need for a thoughtful and methodical response to Russia's actions, acknowledging the ongoing cooperation with the Trump team for a smooth transition. Obama's comments reflect his moral stance on the issue, highlighting his concern for foreign influence in elections and the importance of taking a stand against autocratic leaders.
