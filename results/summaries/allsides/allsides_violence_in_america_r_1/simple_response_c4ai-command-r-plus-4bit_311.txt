SUMMARY: Attorney General William Barr announced 51 arrests related to violent rioting during the protests over the death of George Floyd. Barr condemned the rioting and looting, distinguishing between peaceful protesters and those who hijack protests to pursue their own agendas, including extremist groups like Antifa and foreign actors. He acknowledged the legitimate concerns of protesters about police misconduct and injustices in the criminal justice system, pledging to find solutions while also upholding the need for law and order. Barr defended President Trump's visit to St. John's Church as appropriate and non-political, stating the president should be able to walk outside the White House and visit the church.

END OF SUMMARY.
