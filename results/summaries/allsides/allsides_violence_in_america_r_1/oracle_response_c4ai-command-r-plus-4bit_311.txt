SUMMARY: Attorney General William Barr announced 51 arrests for federal crimes related to violent rioting during protests over the death of George Floyd. Barr condemned the acts of violence, arson, looting, and assaults as crimes designed to terrify and intimidate communities. He distinguished between peaceful protesters exercising their First Amendment rights and extremist agitators hijacking the protests to pursue their own agendas. Barr acknowledged concerns about police misconduct and injustices in the criminal justice system, pledging to find constructive solutions. He defended President Trump's visit to St. John's Church as appropriate, denying it was a political act. The Justice Department is working with other agencies to restore order and quell violence. Barr emphasized the need for equal protection under the law for all Americans.

END OF SUMMARY.
