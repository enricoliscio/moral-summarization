SUMMARY: Attorney General William Barr has announced 51 arrests for federal crimes related to violent rioting during the protests over the death of George Floyd. Barr condemned the violent and destructive acts of rioters, distinguishing them from peaceful protesters exercising their First Amendment rights. He acknowledged the legitimate concerns of protesters regarding police misconduct and injustices in the criminal justice system, pledging to work towards constructive solutions. Barr also addressed criticisms of President Trump's visit to St. John's Church, stating that it was appropriate for the President to walk to the church and that it was not a political act. The Attorney General emphasized the presence of extremist groups, like Antifa, and foreign actors exacerbating the violence. He vowed that the Justice Department would continue its efforts to restore order and hold accountable those responsible for the rioting and looting. 

END OF SUMMARY.
