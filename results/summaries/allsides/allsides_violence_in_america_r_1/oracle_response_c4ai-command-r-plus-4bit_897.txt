SUMMARY: Attorney General William Barr announced 51 arrests for federal crimes related to violent rioting during protests over the death of George Floyd. Barr condemned the violent and lawless acts, distinguishing them from peaceful protests. He attributed the violence to extremist agitators hijacking the demonstrations for their own agendas, including Antifa and foreign actors. Barr acknowledged concerns about police misconduct and injustices in the criminal justice system, pledging to find solutions. He defended President Trump's visit to St. John's Church as appropriate and unrelated to the clearing of protesters from Lafayette Park. Barr emphasized the need for equal protection under the law for all Americans.

Barr's statement highlights the challenges faced by law enforcement in balancing the rights of peaceful protesters with the need to restore order and prevent further violence, rioting, and injustices.

END OF SUMMARY.
