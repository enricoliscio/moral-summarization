SUMMARY: Fox is allowing Nick Cannon to remain on "The Masked Singer" despite his anti-Semitic and anti-white comments made during a podcast episode with Professor Griff, who has a history of anti-Semitic remarks. Fox accepted Cannon's apology, recognizing his remorse and willingness to educate himself and make amends. Fox aims to move forward with Cannon and foster a dialogue to combat bigotry and hate. In contrast, ViacomCBS terminated its relationship with Cannon, citing his failure to acknowledge and apologize for perpetuating anti-Semitism. Cannon initially refused to apologize, demanding an apology from ViacomCBS instead. He eventually issued an apology, expressing remorse for his hurtful words and committing to educating himself and strengthening ties between cultures. Prominent figures have supported Cannon's decision to take time off from his radio show to focus on reflection and education.
