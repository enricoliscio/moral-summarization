SUMMARY: Nick Cannon made anti-Semitic and anti-white comments during an episode of his podcast, "Cannon's Class," sparking backlash and consequences from media companies. Fox has decided to keep Cannon on "The Masked Singer," while ViacomCBS terminated its relationship with him. Cannon initially refused to apologize but eventually issued a statement expressing remorse and committing to education and dialogue. He will be taking time away from his morning radio show to focus on reflection and learning. Prominent figures like Charlamagne tha God and Sean 'Diddy' Combs have supported Cannon. Fox plans to work with Cannon to promote dialogue and combat bigotry.
END OF SUMMARY.
