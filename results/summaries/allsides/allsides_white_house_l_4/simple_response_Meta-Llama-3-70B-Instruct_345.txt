SUMMARY:

The article reflects on the US foreign policy since 9/11, criticizing the unilateralist excess of the Bush administration and the reactive disengagement of the Obama administration. The author argues that the nation's problems stem from a heightened sense of vulnerability and human errors that can be fixed. The article suggests that Obama can learn from George W. Bush's ability to recognize mistakes and institute change. Bush's second term saw a sweeping reordering of his administration, policies, and priorities, which led to better results in various regions. The author praises Bush's willingness to admit errors and bring in new perspectives, contrasting it with Obama's aloofness from his Cabinet. The article concludes that Obama can grow in office by learning from Bush's example, recognizing mistakes, and leading administration-wide change.

END OF SUMMARY.
