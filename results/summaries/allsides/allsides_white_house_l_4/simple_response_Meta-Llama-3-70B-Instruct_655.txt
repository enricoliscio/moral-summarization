SUMMARY:

The article reflects on the US foreign policy since 9/11, criticizing the unilateralist excess of the Bush administration and the reactive disengagement of the Obama administration. The author argues that the nation's problems stem from a heightened sense of vulnerability and human errors that can be fixed. The article suggests that President Obama can learn from George W. Bush's ability to recognize and correct his mistakes, as seen in his second term. Bush's willingness to admit errors and embrace change led to improved results in various regions, including Iraq, Africa, and relations with allies. The author contrasts Bush's approach with Obama's, highlighting the importance of acknowledging shortcomings and seeking outside perspectives. The article concludes that Obama can grow in office by learning from Bush's example and taking responsibility for his mistakes.
