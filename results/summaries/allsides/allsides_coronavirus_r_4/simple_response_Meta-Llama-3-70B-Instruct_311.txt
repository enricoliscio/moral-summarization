SUMMARY:

A protest in Kentucky turned ugly when demonstrators hung an effigy of Democratic Gov. Andy Beshear outside the state capitol, sparking widespread condemnation from both parties. The rally, organized by Take Back Kentucky, aimed to "celebrate freedom" and fight against coronavirus restrictions. However, the event took a dark turn when protesters marched to the governor's house and later hung the effigy, which featured a picture of Beshear's face and a message that translates to "thus always to tyrants." The actions were swiftly denounced by prominent figures, including Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, who called the display "unacceptable" and said "there is no place for hate in Kentucky." Other officials, including Republican Secretary of State Michael G. Adams and Democratic Louisville Mayor Greg Fischer, also strongly condemned the effigy, with Fischer tweeting that it was a "vile act" that undermined the sacrifices made to protect the community from the pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
